Claude Code agents have gotten really good.

So good, in fact, that the agents aren't the bottleneck anymore. You are.

Here's what I mean:

You start a task. Claude's running autonomously for 5-10 minutes. Your mind wanders. You open another terminal. Then another. Before you know it, you've got 5 tabs open and you're clicking between them going "which one was building my landing page?"

Every time you switch, you lose context. That mental overhead adds up fast.

So I went looking for solutions. Tried everything:

- Tmux: See multiple sessions, but you're still stuck in terminals
- Anthropic's desktop app: Nicer UI, still one conversation at a time
- Vibe Kanban: Brilliant for developers, but it speaks GitHub commits and pull requests
- Paperclip: An "autonomous company OS" — way too much for writing a LinkedIn post and building a landing page

Every tool out there solves the same problem: "How do I manage multiple coding sessions?"

But that's the wrong question for business owners.

The right question: "How do I manage multiple business goals and let Claude figure out the rest?"

So I built a command center on top of our Agentic OS. Think of it as a kanban board — but for goals, not code.

Instead of "not started / in progress / done," it mirrors the iterative reality of working with agents: Your Turn vs Claude's Turn.

You describe a goal. Choose the depth (quick task, campaign, or deep build). Hit send. Claude figures out the planning, the agents, and the execution.

You stay one layer above the terminal. Reviewing outputs. Giving feedback. Managing 6+ tasks at a glance.

It also handles scheduled tasks, skills management, multi-client support, and keeps your full business context (brand voice, ICPs, content strategy) baked in — not operating in a vacuum.

The terminal was fine when we ran one session at a time.

But agents have levelled up. Now we need to level up how we manage them.

Stop managing terminals. Start managing goals.
