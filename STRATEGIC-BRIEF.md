## Strategic Decisions Made

### 1. Focus: Refining Labs, Not Building Something New
We are taking the existing Labs three-phase system (Map, Build, Run) and adapting it for the medical spa vertical. The flywheel, Magnolia platform, and Studios/Capital units remain the same.

### 2. Offer Structure: Entry Points + Full Engagement
Both approaches. Small, targeted offers that deliver quick wins AND a full engagement as the upsell.

- **Entry-point offers** (low ticket): Quick-win automations that deliver ROI in 30 days or less. Purpose: prove Allenix works, build trust, create a relationship.
- **Core offers** (mid ticket): Bundled solutions that solve a major pain area (acquisition, retention, efficiency). Purpose: meaningful monthly revenue, deeper integration.
- **Full engagement** (high ticket): The complete Map, Build, Run system. Purpose: transformational outcome, platform subscription revenue.

### 3. Ranking Framework: Bang for the Buck
Every opportunity and offer must be evaluated by:
- **Effort** to implement (time, complexity, disruption)
- **Impact** delivered (revenue, time savings, cost reduction)
- **Speed to results** (days/weeks to see ROI)
- **Risk** level (what could go wrong)

Priority: lowest effort, highest impact, fastest results, lowest risk.

### 4. GTM Targets
- 3 new clients per month
- ~$5K-$15K MRR per client
- ~1,200 ICP contacts reached per month
- 12 qualified conversations per month
- 25% close rate (3 of 12)

## The Grand Slam Offer Approach (Alex Hormozi Framework)

### Value Equation
Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement / Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice

For med spa owners, this means:
- **Dream Outcome**: Every patient is worth 2-3x more because they actually come back. Full schedule, consistent revenue, practice that runs without them.
- **Perceived Likelihood**: Must feel inevitable, not hopeful. Use proof from similar practices.
- **Time Delay**: Must be fast. 30 days or less for first result.
- **Effort**: Must be minimal on their end. Allenix does the heavy lifting.

### Offer Principles
1. The offer must feel like a "shut up and take my money" proposition
2. Perceived value must be at least 10x the price
3. Risk reversal is critical (guarantees that remove fear)
4. Entry point must be easy (low friction, fast decision)
5. Ascension path must be obvious (each tier leads naturally to the next)

## Top Quick-Win Opportunities (Validated by Research)

Based on deep research, ranked by effort vs impact. Retention/LTV levers are ranked first because the math is overwhelming — most practices are hemorrhaging patients they already paid to acquire.

1. **Automated rebooking + post-care sequences** — Up to 80% of ad-driven first-visit patients never return. Automated treatment-specific follow-up (neurotoxin at 90 days, laser at 4-6 weeks) increases 90-day retention by 10-20%. ReEnvision Med Spa recovered $8-10K/month just from patients who forgot to rebook. Effort: low. Cost: $50-150/mo in tools.
2. **No-show prevention** — Practices lose up to $215K/year to missed appointments. Automated SMS reminders reduce no-shows from 17-22% down to 8-12%. NoLo case study: Radiant Med Spa hit $75K/month by preventing no-shows, system paid for itself in 4 weeks.
3. **Automated review + referral generation** — 79-84% of patients read reviews before choosing a provider. Automated 24-hour post-treatment review requests (with smart filtering for negative experiences) drive massive SEO gains. BeVibrant generated 22 qualified referrals in a single week using SurgePoint.
4. **AI phone receptionist** — 37% of calls go unanswered during peak hours. AI reception captures 95% of missed calls. Chicago med spa: 22% increase in monthly bookings, 380% ROI in 6 months.
5. **Win-back campaigns for dormant patients** — One practice found 65% of their database had been dormant 6+ months, representing $590K in recoverable revenue. Targeted SMS campaigns (98% open rate vs 37% for email) reactivate lapsed patients at a fraction of new patient acquisition cost ($610 avg).

## Key Constraints

- HIPAA compliance is non-negotiable for any patient-facing automation
- Must integrate with existing practice management tools (PatientNow, Zenoti, Mindbody)
- Implementation must be dead simple (front desk adoption is the #1 risk)
- Must deliver measurable ROI within 30 days for entry-point offers
- Owner cannot be required to do significant work during implementation
- Must work for practices with 5-50 employees
