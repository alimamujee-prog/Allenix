We work with two people in every business.
Both of them are exhausted.

The founder carrying the strategy. And the operator holding everything together underneath. If either of these sounds familiar, you're in the right place.

The Founder / CEO
"I know AI matters. I don't have time to figure out what to actually do with it."
  • You've bought the tools. The team uses them. Your numbers haven't moved.
  • Your Vistage peer says he saved $400K with AI. You nodded. You're not sure it's real.
  • Your board or PE sponsor wants "an AI story." You don't have one you believe in yet.
  • You've been burned before — a consultant, a freelancer, a Big 4 deck. $150K for a PDF.
  • Every decision still routes through you. That was fine at $5M. It's a trap at $20M.
  • You built this company for 15 years. You're not going to blow it up chasing a trend.
The Operator / Susan
"I'm the one who actually has to make this work — and nobody asked me what I need."
  • You've been here 12 years. You know where every body is buried.
  • The last software rollout landed in your lap with no training and a broken manual.
  • You're the only person who truly understands how this business runs day to day.
  • Every AI pitch goes to the CEO. Nobody talks to you until it's time to implement.
  • You don't need a chatbot. You need the Friday AR report to stop being a 3-hour job.
  • If it doesn't work for you, it doesn't work. Full stop.
