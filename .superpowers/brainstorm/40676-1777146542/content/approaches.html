Three Draft Structures

All three share the same core: make the founder AND Susan see themselves, then show the Allenix partnership. Pick the narrative arc that fits.

A

The Mirror

Arc: See yourself → Feel heard → Trust us → Join us


Sections:

  1. Hero — bold statement about where they are right now
  2. Letter from Ali — short, direct, operator to operator
  3. "Does this sound like you?" — 6 pain points (founder + Susan both named)
  4. "Here's what we do" — in-person, immediate value, partner framing
  5. "Your journey with us" — 3 stages showing their transformation
  6. CTA — one ask

Best for: emotional resonance first, process second. Closest to BasicArts structure.

B

The Indictment

Arc: Name the problem plainly → Call out what doesn't work → Offer the alternative


Sections:

  1. Hero — the specific situation they're in (numbers, not adjectives)
  2. "What's actually broken" — 4 truths about their business right now
  3. Letter from Ali — positioned as contrast to consultants
  4. "Why we're different" — in-person, immediate value, founder + team model
  5. "What changes" — before/after framing for founder AND Susan
  6. Who this is for — self-selection filter
  7. CTA

Best for: trust-building. Leads with the industry's failures before presenting Allenix. Strongest anti-consultant positioning.

C

The Two Characters

Arc: Name the founder AND Susan explicitly → Show you understand both → Show how the partnership covers both


Sections:

  1. Hero — addresses the founder-and-team dynamic directly
  2. Letter from Ali
  3. "The founder" — pain portrait of the CEO
  4. "The Susan" — pain portrait of the key operator
  5. "What working with us looks like" — in-person, immediate, both characters shown
  6. Outcomes — wins described in their language
  7. CTA

Best for: differentiation. No other AI firm talks about the operator layer explicitly. Makes Susan feel seen — and that makes the founder trust you.

My recommendation: C — The two-character structure is the most differentiated. Every AI firm pitches the CEO. Nobody pitches the Susan. That's exactly the in-person, partner-not-consultant positioning that Allenix stands for. And it directly reflects what Dustin and Ali described: work with the founder AND the Susan to understand the business.
