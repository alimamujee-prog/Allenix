# Prompt 1: Medical Spa Owner Psychology & Buying Behavior

Research the psychology, buying behavior, and decision-making patterns of medical spa and esthetic practice owners who generate $1M-$10M annual revenue. I need to understand:

## 1. Owner Demographics & Background
- Typical age, gender, professional background (NP, MD, RN, entrepreneur)
- How long they've owned their practice
- What led them to start/own a practice vs being an employee

## 2. What Keeps Them Up At Night
- Top 5-7 specific business worries (beyond generic "cash flow")
- What they're personally stressed about in their day-to-day
- Problems they've tried to solve but failed
- Where they feel they're losing money that they can't fix

## 3. Buying Decision Patterns
- How they discover and evaluate new services/software
- What triggers them to say "I need this now" vs "maybe later"
- How much research they do before buying
- Who they trust for recommendations (peers, industry publications, etc.)
- Price sensitivity and how they justify purchases

## 4. Current Frustrations
- Manual tasks they hate doing
- Problems they've accepted as "just part of the business"
- Where they feel technology has let them down
- Competitors they're losing to and why

## 5. Desired Outcomes
- What their ideal practice looks like in 12-24 months
- Personal goals (exit, lifestyle, growth, etc.)
- What would make them feel "successful"

## Research Focus
Focus on primary research, surveys, interviews, and industry reports. Include specific quotes and anecdotes if available. Look for data from:
- American Med Spa Association (AmSpa)
- Medical Spa Show conference proceedings
- Industry surveys and reports
- Practice owner interviews and profiles
- Healthcare entrepreneurship research

## Output Format
Return findings with specific data points, percentages, quotes, and real examples. Cite sources where possible.
