## The Bowtie: Medical Spa Customer Lifecycle

> Framework from WbD Revenue Architecture. The Bowtie replaces the traditional sales funnel with a model that covers the full customer lifecycle — acquisition through expansion. Every Allenix assessment call should diagnose which Bowtie stages are leaking and attach a dollar number to each gap.

### Left Side (Acquisition)

| Metric | Stage | What It Measures | Industry Benchmark | Top Performer |
|--------|-------|------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| VM1 | Prospects | People exposed to the practice (search, social, referral, drive-by) | — | — |
| VM2 | Leads | Website visitors, DMs, call-inquiries, form fills | — | — |
| CR1 | Awareness | Lead-to-inquiry conversion rate | 8-12% | 20%+ |
| CR2 | Education | Inquiry-to-consultation booked rate | — | — |
| VM3 | Qualified | Consultations booked | — | — |
| CR3 | Prioritization | Consultation show-up rate | 85% | 90%+ |
| CR4 | Selection | Consult-to-treatment conversion rate | 30-40% | 60-70% |

### Center (Commit)

| Metric | Stage | What It Measures | Industry Benchmark |
|--------|-------|------------------|--------------------|
| VM5 | Commit | First treatment completed, first payment collected | — |
| delta-t5 | Commit Time | Days from first inquiry to first treatment | 7-14 days |

### Right Side (Retention + Expansion)

| Metric | Stage | What It Measures | Industry Benchmark | Top Performer |
|--------|-------|------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| CR6 | Onboarding | % of first-visit patients who rebook before leaving | 39% | 74% |
| CR7 | Retention (GRR) | % who return within standard treatment cycle (90 days neurotoxin, 4-6 weeks laser) | 50% | 60-80% |
| CR8 | Expansion (NRR) | % who add services, buy retail, upgrade to membership | 15-20% | 30%+ |
| VM9 | LTV | Total revenue per patient over the full relationship | $5,000-$15,000 | $15,000+ |

### Key Insight

Most practices have zero visibility past VM5. They know how many patients they acquired. They don't know how many came back, how much each is worth, or where in the lifecycle they're losing them. The right side of the Bowtie is where compound revenue lives — and where Allenix makes its case.

Every assessment call should produce a rough Bowtie for that practice. The gap between their numbers and top-performer benchmarks is the revenue Allenix can recover.

## Patient Journey

### 1. Consultation and Booking
- Manual phone calls and voicemail tag
- Front desk spending significant time on scheduling
- Inconsistent consultation preparation
- No pre-consultation intake automation
- Key friction: "I called but no one answered", "I waited on hold", "I had to re-explain my info"

### 2. Pre-Treatment Communication
- Manual reminder calls (time-consuming, inconsistent)
- Generic prep instructions sent to all patients
- Last-minute cancellation notices too late to fill slots
- Potential wins: automated reminders, personalized prep sequences

### 3. Post-Treatment Follow-Up
- Inconsistent follow-up timing
- Generic post-care instructions
- No proactive complication monitoring
- Missed re-booking opportunities
- Manual review requests
- **This is a retention/LTV lever** — validate how much revenue is lost here

### 4. Re-Booking and Retention
- Passive re-booking (waiting for patients to call back)
- Inconsistent retention sequences
- No churn prediction or prevention
- Generic re-treatment reminders
- **Right side of the bowtie** — likely the biggest unrealized revenue opportunity

### 5. Payment and Collections
- Manual payment processing
- Time-consuming collections calls
- Passive failed payment handling
- No automated payment plan management
- Chargeback losses

## Administrative Bottlenecks

### 1. Appointment Scheduling and Reminders
- Manual calendar management, phone/email tag
- No intelligent waitlist management
- Poor utilization analytics

### 2. Intake Forms and Medical History
- Paper forms or basic digital forms with manual data entry
- Incomplete medical histories, redundant data collection
- No automated risk screening

### 3. Inventory Management
- Manual tracking, stockouts disrupting schedules
- Overstocking tying up capital
- Expiration date management issues

### 4. Staff Scheduling
- Manual schedule creation, inefficient shift assignments
- Time-consuming substitution management

### 5. Reviews and Reputation
- Passive review collection, inconsistent request timing
- No systematic response to negative reviews
- Manual monitoring across platforms

## Marketing & Sales

### 1. Lead Generation
- Passive lead capture, no lead scoring
- Slow response times, poor follow-up
- Untrackable marketing spend

### 2. Consultation Conversion
- Manual prep, inconsistent materials
- No follow-up automation after consultation
- Poor conversion tracking

### 3. Email/SMS Marketing
- Generic blast campaigns, no segmentation
- Manual list management, poor timing

### 4. Social Media
- Manual content creation, inconsistent posting
- Time-intensive, hard to measure ROI

### 5. Referral Programs
- Manual tracking, no automated thank-you sequences
- Poor visibility into referral sources

## Industry Context

### Medical Spa vs. General Spa
- Medical supervision requirements, HIPAA compliance
- More complex intake and consent processes
- Higher average ticket, longer treatment cycles
- More pronounced no-show financial impact

### Texas / Gulf South Considerations
- Lower competition than coastal markets
- Price-sensitive but value-conscious patients
- Strong referral culture
- Growing market with new practices opening

## Offer Bundle Concepts (Starting Points)

These are directional — pricing and positioning should be refined after research comes back from P1-P3 prompts.

### Bundle 1: "Patient Acquisition Accelerator"
- AI phone receptionist, instant lead response, automated review generation, behavioral email/SMS marketing

### Bundle 2: "Revenue Recovery System"
- No-show prevention, automated payment recovery, intelligent waitlist, cancellation policy automation

### Bundle 3: "Patient Retention Engine"
- Automated post-treatment follow-up, churn prediction, smart re-booking, loyalty programs
- **Note:** This is likely the most differentiated bundle. Most competitors sell acquisition. Few sell retention.

### Bundle 4: "Operational Efficiency Platform"
- Digital intake, automated reminders, AI scribe for notes, automated review requests

## What Needs Validation

Before finalizing offers, we need real answers on:
- Actual no-show rates and revenue impact (not estimates)
- Patient retention rates at 30, 90, 180 days
- Average patient lifetime value
- What owners currently spend on marketing/tech
- Which pain points they'll actually pay to solve vs. tolerate
- Whether owners even track retention/LTV metrics
