# Prompt 3: Medical Spa Operational Pain Points & Time Leaks

Research the specific operational pain points, time leaks, and manual workflows that plague medical spa and aesthetic practice operations. I need to understand:

## 1. Staffing & Training Pain Points
- How long it takes to onboard new aestheticians/injectors
- What training burden falls on the owner vs senior staff
- Turnover rates and costs of replacing staff
- What compliance/documentation work overwhelms them

## 2. Patient Communication Bottlenecks
- How much time front desk spends on phone/email daily
- What percentage of calls go unanswered or go to voicemail
- How long it takes to respond to patient inquiries
- What follow-up tasks fall through cracks
- Manual work around reminders, confirmations, rebooking

## 3. Financial & Administrative Leaks
- No-show and cancellation rates and revenue impact
- How much revenue is lost to poor payment collection
- Inventory challenges (expired products, stockouts)
- Billing and insurance delays
- Time spent on financial reporting and what they can't see

## 4. Marketing & Acquisition Challenges
- How much they spend monthly on marketing and what channels
- What marketing activities they can't track or measure
- How they handle leads and response times
- Review generation and reputation management workload
- Social media and content creation burden

## 5. Specific Time Studies (if available)
- How many hours per week are lost to specific manual tasks
- What tasks owners do themselves because they can't delegate
- Where they know they're inefficient but haven't solved it

## Research Focus
Focus on specific, quantified data points. Include real examples and case studies if possible. Look for:
- Time studies and workflow analyses
- Industry benchmarks on operational metrics
- Practice management software data
- Staffing surveys and turnover studies
- Financial benchmark reports

## Output Format
Return findings with:
- Specific hours/days lost to each task
- Revenue impact of each pain point
- Frequency (how often it occurs)
- Severity (how much it hurts)
- Real quotes from practice owners if available

## Priority Areas
Prioritize findings that have clear dollar values or time savings attached.
