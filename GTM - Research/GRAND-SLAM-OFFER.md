## Framework: Alex Hormozi's Value Equation

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Maximize the numerator. Minimize the denominator. The offer should feel like a "shut up and take my money" proposition.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Starving Crowd

Med spa owners in the $2M–$8M range are starving for this because:

1. **They know they're losing money but can't see where.** 80% of first-visit patients never return. The owner knows patients "disappear" but has no system to stop it.
2. **They've been burned by agencies.** They've paid $3K–$5K/month for social media posts that didn't move revenue. They're skeptical but desperate.
3. **They can't hire their way out.** Adding another front desk person doesn't fix the follow-up gap. They've tried.
4. **Competition is accelerating.** A new med spa opens every day. The window to build a defensible practice is closing.
5. **Retention is the highest-leverage play they're NOT making.** They spend 80–100% of marketing budget on acquisition and 0% on retention. The math is embarrassing once they see it.

This is a starving crowd because they have the money, they have the pain, and they don't have a solution. They're not browsing. They're bleeding.

## Step 3: Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (Numerator Part 2)

The offer must feel inevitable, not hopeful. Build perceived likelihood with:

### Specific Proof
- ReEnvision Med Spa: "$8–10K/month recovered from patients who forgot to rebook"
- Automated reminders reduce no-shows from 17–22% to 8–12%
- 90-day retention increases 10–20% with treatment-specific follow-up
- SMS has 98% open rate vs 37% for email

### Mechanism Clarity
Don't say "we'll improve your retention." Say:
1. We map your top 5 treatments and their natural rebooking windows (neurotoxin at 90 days, laser at 4–6 weeks, filler at 6–9 months)
2. We build automated SMS sequences for each window
3. We add post-treatment check-ins at 24–48 hours
4. We set up no-show prevention (reminders at 48hr, 24hr, 2hr)
5. We trigger review requests 24 hours after positive visits
6. All of it runs inside your existing practice management software — no new tools for your staff

The more specific the mechanism, the higher the perceived likelihood.

### "Like You" Proof
- "We've done this for practices doing $2M–$8M in revenue, with 8–25 employees, using [their exact PM software]."
- Name the practice management software they use. This signals deep understanding.

## Step 5: Minimize Effort & Sacrifice (Denominator Part 2)

This is where most competitors fail. They sell software the owner has to set up and manage. Allenix does the opposite.

**Owner effort required:**
- One 45-minute onboarding call
- Approve message templates (review, not write)
- That's it

**Front desk effort required:**
- None. Zero new software. The automation runs inside their existing PM system.

**What the owner sacrifices:**
- Nothing. No behavior change. No new tool. No training.
- The system works behind the scenes.

**What the owner does NOT sacrifice:**
- The personal touch with patients (automation handles follow-up, not the clinical relationship)
- Control (she approves every message template)
- Money (the guarantee covers her)
