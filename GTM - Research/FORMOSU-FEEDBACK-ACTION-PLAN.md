## Bottom Line

Formosu's core read: the strategy is sound, but the front-end offer needs to feel like a category-of-one paid diagnostic, not a strategic audit.

The fix is to make the first yes very specific:

**Patient Revenue Recovery Audit**

"In 14 days, we map where $150K-$750K/year is leaking across your locations and give you a 90-day plan to recover it using the tools you already pay for. If we do not find at least 5x your fee in provable recoverable annual revenue, you do not pay."

---

## Decisions

### 1. Standardize on Zenoti and PatientNow first

Lead with: **"We make Zenoti and PatientNow actually make you money."**

Why:
- It is a cleaner hook than "we work with your practice management software."
- It makes implementation repeatable.
- It gives outreach a sharper filter.
- It lets Allenix become known for a specific operational outcome inside the systems larger med spas already use.

Mindbody stays secondary. It can be served when the account is otherwise excellent, but it should not be the first-positioning wedge.

### 2. Ali should take the first 10-15 Checkup calls

Ali should run the first calls personally.

Why:
- The offer still needs founder-led pattern recognition.
- The best copy will come from the exact objections owners use on calls.
- Early calls are product research, not just sales.
- The first 3 audits need enough trust to get data access and case-study rights.

No closer until:
- The Checkup script has converted at least 3 paid audits.
- There are 10+ recorded calls with objections tagged.
- The audit close rate and implementation conversion rate are known.

### 3. Discount the first 3 Audits only for data and case-study rights

Offer:

**Founding Patient Revenue Recovery Audit: $3,800 instead of $4,800**

Only available if they agree to:
- Share anonymized before/after metrics.
- Allow a named case study if the work produces agreed results.
- Provide a 30-second owner quote or short written testimonial.
- Make one peer introduction if the audit is valuable.

Do not discount the Build-Out. The audit discount buys proof. The implementation price anchors value.

## Funnel Change

Use one CTA everywhere:

**Book a 30-minute Patient Revenue Checkup.**

The Checkup exists to:
1. Confirm revenue, locations, and platform.
2. Calculate rough retention math live.
3. Show 3-5 likely leaks by location.
4. Close the paid Audit on the call.

Avoid presenting all three tiers on the first call. Mention the Build-Out only as the natural next step after the audit:

"If the roadmap is obvious and you want us to execute it, the audit fee credits toward the build-out."

## Landing Page Direction

Above the fold:

**Multi-location med spas are leaking $150K-$750K/year in missed visits and no-shows.**

**We find it and fix it inside Zenoti and PatientNow.**

CTA:

**Book your Patient Revenue Checkup**

Required sections:
- The leak: missed visits, no-shows, one-and-done patients, dropped plans.
- The audit: 14 days, $4,800, 5x or no pay.
- The deliverables: Money Leak Map, 90-day Retention Sprint, 1-page owner / lender summary.
- The proof: industry case studies until Allenix has client proof.
- The compliance reassurance: HIPAA-safe appointment and care-follow-up messaging.
- The next step: Checkup call.

Tighten:

## Copy Rules For This Vertical

Say:
- "Recovered visits."
- "Missed revenue."
- "Patients you already paid to acquire."
- "Inside the software your team already uses."
- "Location-by-location."
- "No new software for the front desk."

Avoid:
- "Strategy."
- "Transformation" on the front end.
- "AI implementation."
- "Automation platform."
- "Marketing engine" before trust is earned.
