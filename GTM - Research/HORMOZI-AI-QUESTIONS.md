## Context to Feed the AI First

Before asking questions, give the Hormozi AI this context:

**Business:** Allenix helps $5M–$15M multi-location medical spa owners fix patient retention and operational efficiency. We implement automated follow-up, rebooking, no-show prevention, review generation, AI receptionist, and referral systems inside their existing practice management software. No new tools for the staff. Done-for-you implementation. Results in 2–4 weeks.

**ICP:** "Priya" — a 45-year-old nurse practitioner or physician who owns a $5M–$15M multi-location med spa group with 2–5 locations and 20–100 employees. She started as the top injector and became the CEO by necessity. No CMO, no COO. Her office managers run the day-to-day at each location, but nobody owns the systems across locations. She's been burned by agencies. She trusts peers, not pitches. She thinks in monthly revenue, not LTV. Multi-location complexity is her biggest pain — fragmented scheduling, inconsistent follow-up, no cross-location visibility.

**The Problem:** 80% of first-visit patients never return. Owners spend 100% of marketing budget on acquisition, 0% on retention. A $5M practice at 50% retention leaves ~$1M/year on the table. Multiplied by each location, the losses compound. She can't see what's happening at Location 2 when she's at Location 1.

**The Offer Structure (3 Tiers):**

1. **Patient Revenue Recovery Audit** ($4,800 one-time): Full diagnostic of patient data, location-by-location Money Leak Map, retention math, competitive benchmark, tech stack audit, 90-day Retention Sprint roadmap with dollar amounts, and 1-page owner / lender summary. 14-day delivery. 5x or no-pay guarantee. Entry point.

2. **Retention Engine Build-Out** ($8,000/month for 3 months, ramping to $10,000–$15,000): Execute the top 3-5 roadmap plays. Retention engine, revenue protection, reputation management, patient reactivation, basic AI receptionist, referral engine. Done-for-you. 3-month minimum. Performance guarantee in 90 days. Owner involvement: 90-min kickoff + weekly 15-min review.

3. **Self-Running Med Spa Group Program** ($15,000/month): Full end-to-end. Acquisition optimization, operational automation, cross-location dashboard, membership / loyalty buildout, dedicated success manager, Studios content tied to retention campaigns. 6-month minimum. Revenue and owner-time target guarantee.

## Section 1: Grand Slam Offer Creation

### 1.1 Value Equation Optimization

1. I have a 3-tier offer: $3.5–5K diagnostic entry, $5–15K/mo core engagement, $10–15K/mo full transformation. Using the Value Equation (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood / Time Delay × Effort), which tier is the weakest offer? Where should I focus refinement first?

2. My dream outcome for the Practice Audit is: "See exactly where $50K–$200K/year is leaking, with a dollar-quantified roadmap to recover it." For Implementation: "Recover $15K–$50K/month in lost revenue across all locations." Are these specific enough for a skeptical 45-year-old NP who's been burned by agencies? How would you sharpen them?

3. The time delay for the Audit is 2 weeks. For Implementation, first recovered revenue hits in 2–4 weeks. Is that fast enough for a $5–8K/month commitment? Could I credibly promise first revenue in 7 days on the Implementation? What would I need to deliver?

4. My effort minimization is: "90-minute call for the Audit, weekly 15-minute reviews for Implementation, monthly strategy call for Transformation. No new software. No staff training." What else could I remove from the owner's plate to make this feel even more effortless?

5. The 3-month minimum on Implementation is a friction point. A month-to-month option feels easier to say yes to, but implementation takes time to prove ROI. How do I frame the commitment as a benefit, not a trap?

### 1.2 Starving Crowd Validation

6. My starving crowd is: multi-location med spa owners losing 50%+ of patients after first visit, no retention system, spending all marketing budget on acquisition, can't see what's happening across locations. On a scale of 1–10, how hungry is this crowd? What would make them hungrier?

7. I'm targeting owners doing $5M–$15M revenue with 2–5 locations. Is this the right revenue band? Should I go smaller ($2M–$5M, more desperate, more practices, but less budget per engagement) or larger ($15M–$30M, more budget, but more sophisticated buyers with more options)?

8. The four biggest competitors to hiring us are: (1) do nothing, (2) hire a marketing agency for $3K–$5K/month for social media posts, (3) buy software their staff won't use, (4) promote an office manager to "figure it out." How do I position against all four simultaneously?

9. Is "multi-location" the right qualifying filter? Single-location owners have the same retention problem but less complexity. Should I serve both, or is the multi-location specificity part of what makes the offer feel like it's "for them"?

### 1.3 Pricing and Value Stack

10. My Implementation value stack shows $11,800–$24,500/month in standalone value for a $5,000–$8,000/month price. That's roughly 2–3x. Is that enough multiplier for a Grand Slam, or should I aim for 5–10x? What's the sweet spot for a done-for-you service at this price point?

11. Should I start Implementation pricing at $5K/month or $8K/month? The lower price is an easier first yes. The higher price signals more value and covers costs better. What does $100M Offers say about pricing anchors for a $5M–$15M revenue buyer?

12. The Practice Audit is $3,500–$5,000. Some owners may balk at paying for a diagnostic when they're used to free consultations from agencies. Should I frame it differently? Reduce it? Make it free with a commitment to Implementation? What's the best structure for an entry diagnostic at this price?

13. The Implementation ramps from $5K–$8K/month to $10K–$15K/month over time. How should I present the ramp? Should I quote the starting price and let results justify increases? Or should I show the full range upfront to set expectations?

14. How should I present the value stack on a diagnostic call vs. on a landing page vs. in a proposal? Should the numbers be the same in all three contexts?

## Section 2: Guarantee and Risk Reversal

### 2.1 Guarantee Design

15. My Audit guarantee: "If the audit doesn't identify at least 5x the audit fee in recoverable revenue, the audit is free." My Implementation guarantee: "If the system doesn't generate 5x your monthly fee in recovered revenue within 90 days, we work for half price until it does." Are these strong enough? What would a Grand Slam guarantee look like for each tier?

16. Should I offer a full money-back guarantee on the Practice Audit? The risk is low ($3.5–5K) but she's been burned before. A "pay nothing if you're not satisfied" guarantee might be more powerful than the 5x guarantee for overcoming initial skepticism. Which is stronger?

17. For Implementation, I have three layers: (1) 5x performance guarantee, (2) cancel after 3-month minimum with 30 days notice, (3) Allenix Promise (10x value or we fix/end). Should I add a "first month risk-free" layer, or is that too many guarantees? How many is too many?

18. The Transformation has a revenue target guarantee: "Set specific targets at onboarding. Miss them, engagement extends free." Is this better than a percentage-based guarantee? What's the most powerful guarantee you've seen for a $10–15K/month done-for-you service?

19. What's the best way to communicate guarantees without sounding desperate? I want them to feel confident, not like I'm overcompensating for something.

### 2.2 Risk Psychology

20. Priya's biggest fear isn't that the system won't work. It's that she'll look foolish for trusting another vendor after being burned by an agency that wasted $15K. How do I address the emotional risk, not just the financial risk?

21. She's afraid her staff won't use "another tool." My system runs inside their existing PM software, but she might not believe that until she sees it. How do I prove zero-disruption before she signs?

22. She worries about patient perception — "will automated texts feel impersonal?" How do I frame automation as enhancing, not replacing, the personal touch she built her reputation on?

23. She worries about HIPAA. Any mention of patient data or automation triggers compliance anxiety. How do I proactively address HIPAA compliance in the pitch without making it the focus?

24. Multi-location owners have an additional fear: "Will this work the same at all my locations?" One location is harder to manage than zero. Three locations with inconsistent adoption is worse than none. How do I address the multi-location implementation risk?

### 5.2 Pitch Language

47. The pitch on diagnostic calls: "We run a Practice Audit — two weeks inside your data. We map every patient touchpoint, calculate the retention math, and deliver a 90-day roadmap with dollar amounts. If we don't find 5x the fee in opportunity, you don't pay." Rate this. Make it sharper.

48. How do I explain "automated retention" without sounding like a software pitch? She doesn't want another tool. She wants patients to come back. How do I make the mechanism invisible and the outcome vivid?

49. When she says "I've tried agencies before and they didn't deliver," what's the exact response? Give me the script. She's been burned by a $3–5K/month agency. I'm asking for $5–8K/month. I need to acknowledge the trauma and position Allenix as categorically different.

50. When she says "how much is this going to cost me" on the diagnostic call, but she really means "is this worth my time and money," how do I answer the question behind the question?

51. How should I talk about the ramp from $5–8K to $10–15K/month? Should I mention it on the first call or save it for the Implementation kickoff? Does upfront transparency build trust or create sticker shock?

### 5.3 Objection Handling

52. "I'm too busy to deal with this right now." She's always going to be busy — that's the symptom of the problem. How do I make the Audit feel like it saves time, not adds to her plate?

53. "My front desk handles follow-up." They don't. They say they do. How do I expose the gap without insulting her team, especially across multiple locations where she can't supervise each front desk?

54. "I need to think about it." What's the real objection she's not saying? And how do I address it without being pushy?

55. "Can you send me some information?" She wants to end the call. How do I keep the conversation going without being aggressive? Or should I let her go and follow up with a specific piece of proof?

56. "My patients come back because they love my work." She believes this. The data says otherwise (80% don't return). How do I show her the gap without making her defensive about her clinical skills?

57. "We already use [Zenoti/PatientNow/Mindbody] for that." She has the tool but doesn't use it fully. How do I position Allenix as "we make your existing investment actually work" without sounding like I'm selling training?

58. "I can't afford $5K/month right now." Is this a price objection or a value objection? How do I tell the difference, and how do I respond to each?

59. "My locations are too different to have one system." She thinks each location needs custom treatment. How do I validate her concern while showing that standardized automation actually scales better?

## How to Use These Questions

**Session 1 — Offer Design (Questions 1–24):** Feed the AI the full offer structure, ICP, and context. Ask questions 1–24 to build and refine the Grand Slam Offer across all three tiers. Focus on value equation optimization, guarantee design, and bonus stacking.

**Session 2 — Messaging and Copy (Questions 44–59):** Feed the AI the finalized offer and Priya's language patterns from the ICP. Ask questions 44–59 to sharpen the pitch, subject lines, and objection handling. Focus on cold outreach language and call scripts.

**Session 3 — Funnel and Scaling (Questions 60–92):** Feed the AI the list building strategy, funnel math, and capacity constraints. Ask questions 60–92 to optimize the conversion path and plan for scale. Focus on call design, landing page, and offer evolution.

**Bonus — Full Dump:** Feed all 92 questions at once with full context and ask the AI to answer every question in sequence, noting which answers it's most confident about and which need real-world validation.
