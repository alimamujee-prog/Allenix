# Questions for the Alex Hormozi AI

## Date
2026-04-23

---

## Context to Feed the AI First

Before asking questions, give the Hormozi AI this context:

**Business:** Allenix helps $2M–$8M medical spa owners fix patient retention using automated follow-up, rebooking, no-show prevention, and review generation. We implement everything inside their existing practice management software. No new tools for the staff. One 45-minute onboarding call. Results in 2 weeks.

**ICP:** "Priya" — 42-year-old NP who owns a $2M–$8M med spa. She's the top injector AND the CEO. No CMO, no COO. She's been burned by marketing agencies. She trusts peers, not pitches. She thinks in monthly revenue, not LTV.

**The Offer:** "Retention Quick Start" — $2,500 setup + $497/month. Automated rebooking for top 5 treatments, no-show prevention, post-treatment follow-up, review generation. 3x guarantee in 60 days or monthly fee is waived.

**The Problem:** 80% of first-visit patients never return. Owners spend 100% of marketing budget on acquisition, 0% on retention. The average owner doesn't know her patient LTV.

---

## Section 1: Grand Slam Offer Creation

### 1.1 Value Equation Optimization

1. Here's my offer for med spa owners: $2,500 setup + $497/mo for an automated patient retention system. Using the Value Equation (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood / Time Delay × Effort), where is this offer weakest? What would you change to make it a true "shut up and take my money" proposition?

2. My dream outcome statement is: "Every patient who walks through your door becomes a long-term client who rebooks on schedule, refers friends, and completes their treatment plan." Is this specific enough? How would you sharpen it for a skeptical 42-year-old nurse practitioner who's been burned by agencies?

3. The time delay in my offer is: first results in 2 weeks, pays for itself in 60 days. Is that fast enough? Could I credibly promise first recovered revenue in 7 days? What would I need to deliver to make that claim honest?

4. My effort minimization is: "one 45-minute call and template approval. No new software." What else could I remove from the owner's plate to make this feel even more effortless?

### 1.2 Starving Crowd Validation

5. My starving crowd is: med spa owners losing 50%+ of patients after first visit, no retention system, spending all marketing budget on acquisition. On a scale of 1-10, how hungry is this crowd? What would make them hungrier?

6. I'm targeting owners doing $2M–$8M revenue. Is this the right revenue band? Should I go smaller ($500K–$2M, more desperate but less budget) or larger ($8M–$20M, more budget but more options)?

7. The three biggest competitors to hiring us are: (1) do nothing, (2) hire a marketing agency for $3K–$5K/month, (3) buy software their staff won't use. How do I position against all three simultaneously?

### 1.3 Pricing and Value Stack

8. My value stack shows $5,300/month in recovered revenue and consulting value for a $497/month price. That's 10.7x. Is 10x the right multiplier, or should I aim higher? What's the sweet spot for this type of offer?

9. Should I charge $497/month or $997/month? The lower price makes it an easy yes. The higher price might signal more value. What does $100M Offers say about pricing anchors for this kind of service?

10. I have a $2,500 setup fee. Some owners might balk at the upfront cost. Should I waive it? Reduce it? Make it contingent on results? What's the best structure for a cash-sensitive buyer?

11. How should I present the value stack on a sales call vs. in a proposal vs. on a landing page? Should the numbers be the same in all three contexts?

## Section 2: Guarantee and Risk Reversal

### 2.1 Guarantee Design

12. My current guarantee: "If the system doesn't recover 3x your monthly fee in 60 days, we waive the monthly fee until it does." Is this strong enough? What would a Grand Slam guarantee look like for this offer?

13. Should I offer a full money-back guarantee on the setup fee? The risk is that someone could take the system, copy it, and ask for a refund. How do I protect against that while still making the guarantee ironclad?

14. I'm considering offering "first month risk-free" — pay setup, if you don't want to continue after 30 days, full refund. Is 30 days the right trial period? What about 14 days? 90 days?

15. How many layers of guarantee is too many? I currently have four: (1) 3x performance guarantee, (2) cancel anytime, (3) 10x value promise, (4) first month risk-free. Should I consolidate or keep all four?

16. What's the most powerful guarantee you've seen for a services business with a monthly retainer? Not SaaS, not productized — a done-for-you service with ongoing delivery.

### 2.2 Risk Psychology

17. Priya's biggest fear isn't that the system won't work. It's that she'll look foolish for trusting another vendor after being burned. How do I address the emotional risk, not just the financial risk?

18. She's also afraid her staff won't use "another tool." My system runs inside their existing software, but she might not believe that. How do I prove zero-disruption before she signs?

19. She worries about patient perception — "will automated texts feel impersonal?" How do I frame automation as enhancing, not replacing, the personal touch?

## Section 3: Bonus Design

### 3.1 Bonus Stacking

20. My current bonuses: (1) Free Practice Assessment ($500), (2) Missed Revenue Calculator ($200), (3) Competitor Benchmark Report ($300), (4) Treatment Rebooking Calendar ($150). Are these the right bonuses? What would make them irresistible?

21. Should the bonuses be available immediately (before signing) to create reciprocity, or only after signing to create incentive? What's the stronger play?

22. What bonuses cost me almost nothing to deliver but have high perceived value to a med spa owner? What would she value that doesn't require me to build anything?

23. Should I offer a "fast action bonus" — extra value if she signs within 48 hours of the assessment call? What would that look like?

24. Is the Competitor Benchmark Report too aggressive? Will it make me look like I'm pitting her against neighbors, or will she see it as valuable intelligence?

### 3.2 Naming and Framing

25. "Retention Quick Start" is the current offer name. Is that compelling? What about "The Patient Recovery System" or "The Rebooking Machine" or "Stop the Bleeding"? What naming approach works best for a skeptical, non-technical buyer?

26. How should I name each bonus? Right now they're descriptive ("Missed Revenue Calculator"). Should they be benefit-named ("Your $640K Recovery Map")?

## Section 4: Offer Structure and Ascension

### 4.1 Entry Offer to Core Offer

27. My entry offer is $497/month. My core offer is $2,500–$4,000/month. The gap is significant. Should I have a middle step? Or is the jump from $497 to $2,500 natural once they see results?

28. What's the best trigger to move someone from Quick Start to Growth System? Is it a time-based trigger (after 60 days), a results-based trigger (after they see 3x return), or an event-based trigger (they ask about other problems)?

29. Should the Quick Start setup fee ($2,500) credit toward the Growth System setup fee ($3,500) to reduce friction on the upgrade? Or does that make the Growth System feel cheaper?

30. How do I prevent Quick Start from becoming the ceiling? Some owners might be happy at $497/month and never ascend. Is that okay, or should the Quick Start be designed to create demand for more?

### 4.2 Full Engagement ($7,500–$15,000/month)

31. For the full Transformation engagement, the 6-month minimum commitment is a significant ask. How do I justify a lock-in when the lower tiers are month-to-month? What language makes this feel like a benefit, not a trap?

32. The Transformation is pitched as "your practice runs itself." Is that the right dream outcome, or is it too abstract? What would a med spa owner find more compelling?

33. How should I present the three tiers on a first call? Do I mention all three, or just pitch the Quick Start and let the other tiers emerge naturally?

## Section 5: Messaging and Copy

### 5.1 Cold Email Subject Lines

34. Rate these cold email subject lines for a med spa owner:
    - "Your patients are disappearing"
    - "Quick question about [practice name]"
    - "12 no-shows last week?"
    - "Found $8K/month you're leaving on the table"
    - "[practice name] vs the 3 med spas within 2 miles"

35. What subject line would get YOU to open an email from a stranger promising to fix your retention?

### 5.2 Pitch Language

36. The pitch I use on calls: "We set up the automated follow-up system that stops your patients from disappearing after their first visit." Rate this. Make it sharper.

37. How do I explain "automated retention" without sounding like a software pitch? She doesn't want another tool. She wants patients to come back. How do I make the mechanism invisible and the outcome vivid?

38. When she says "I've tried marketing agencies before and they didn't deliver," what's the exact response? Give me the script.

39. When she says "how much is this going to cost me," but she really means "is this worth my time," how do I answer the question behind the question?

### 5.3 Objection Handling

40. "I'm too busy to deal with this right now." (She's always going to be busy. How do I make this feel like it saves time, not adds to her plate?)

41. "My front desk handles follow-up." (They don't. They say they do. How do I expose the gap without insulting her team?)

42. "I need to think about it." (What's the real objection she's not saying?)

43. "Can you send me some information?" (She wants to end the call. How do I keep the conversation going without being pushy?)

44. "My patients come back because they love my work." (She believes this. The data says otherwise. How do I show her the gap without making her defensive?)

45. "We already have a system for that." (She has a spreadsheet or a post-it note. How do I differentiate what "system" means?)

## Section 6: Funnel and Conversion

### 6.1 Assessment Call Design

46. My assessment call is 20 minutes. Is that too short to build trust and close? Too long for a busy owner? What's the optimal duration?

47. I calculate the owner's retention math live on the call. The number is usually shocking ($500K–$1M/year in lost recurring revenue). Is the shock value a good thing, or does it make her shut down? How do I present devastating math in a way that creates action, not paralysis?

48. Should the assessment call end with a close, or with booking a second call? Which converts higher — single-call close or two-call close?

### 6.2 Landing Page and Funnel

49. Should the cold email drive to a landing page, or directly to booking a call? Which reduces friction more?

50. What should the landing page say? Should it be long-form (educate on retention math, case studies, then CTA) or short-form (one bold claim, social proof, book now)?

51. Should I offer a lead magnet (free assessment, revenue calculator, competitor report) as an intermediate step, or go straight to call booking? Does the intermediate step increase or decrease total conversions?

## Section 7: Psychology and Positioning

### 7.1 The Buying Psychology

52. Priya makes decisions based on peer proof, not pitches. How do I build peer proof when I don't have clients in this vertical yet? What's the credibility bridge?

53. She trusts people she meets in person more than emails. How do I create a "virtual in-person" feeling in cold outreach? What signals "I'm a real person you can trust"?

54. She's afraid of looking foolish. The last agency she hired wasted $15K and produced nothing. How do I acknowledge that trauma and position Allenix as different without saying "we're not like other agencies"?

55. She doesn't know her patient LTV. Should I educate her on LTV before pitching, or pitch the outcome and let LTV be the mechanism she learns about after signing?

### 7.2 Competitive Positioning

56. Marketing agencies charge $3K–$5K/month for social media management. My Quick Start is $497/month for retention automation. How do I position this as complementary (not competing) with her existing agency spend? Or should I position it as a replacement?

57. Software tools like Zenoti have some built-in automation. How do I position Allenix as "we implement what Zenoti can do but you haven't set up" without sounding like a consultant?

58. Practice management vendors are starting to add AI features. How do I future-proof my positioning against PM vendors building retention features into their platforms?

---

## Section 8: Scaling the Offer

### 8.1 Capacity Planning

59. If I sign 3 clients/month, that's 36 clients in Year 1. At $497–$4,000/month each, that's $18K–$144K MRR. What's the realistic capacity for a small team? When do I need to hire?

60. How much of the Quick Start can be productized vs. needs to be custom for each practice? Where's the line between "customized" and "templated with variables"?

### 8.2 Offer Evolution

61. As I get case studies from real clients, how should the offer evolve? What should change first — the price, the guarantee, the deliverables, or the positioning?

62. Should I create a group coaching / cohort version of the Quick Start at a lower price point to serve owners who can't afford done-for-you? Or does that dilute the brand?

63. The med spa market is growing 14%+ CAGR. In 2–3 years, the market will look different. How do I build an offer that scales with the industry's maturity?

## How to Use These Questions

**Session 1 — Offer Design (Questions 1–19):** Feed the AI the offer structure, ICP, and P1 research. Ask questions 1–19 to build and refine the Grand Slam Offer.

**Session 2 — Messaging and Copy (Questions 34–45):** Feed the AI the finalized offer and ask questions 34–45 to sharpen the pitch, subject lines, and objection handling.

**Session 3 — Funnel and Scaling (Questions 46–63):** Feed the AI the list building strategy and funnel math. Ask questions 46–63 to optimize the conversion path and plan for scale.

**Bonus — Full Dump:** Feed all 63 questions at once with full context and ask the AI to answer every question in sequence, noting which answers it's most confident about and which need real-world validation.
