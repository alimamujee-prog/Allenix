## Strategy

Lead with retention. Everyone sells "more leads." Allenix sells "stop losing the patients you already paid to acquire."

The math is the hook: a $5M practice at 50% retention is leaving ~$1M/year on the table. The fix costs a fraction of that. The front-end offer must be about found money now, not "strategy." Priya should feel like saying no means choosing to keep leaking revenue she already paid to acquire.

---

## Framework: Alex Hormozi's Value Equation

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

Every tier below is designed to maximize the numerator (specific outcome, credible proof) and minimize the denominator (fast results, minimal effort on the owner's part).

## ICP Update

Target practices: **$5M–$15M revenue**, multi-location med spa groups, 20–100 employees, 2–5 locations.

Why this band: a $5M+ practice can sustain a $10–15K/month engagement without it being existential. The retention math is dramatic enough that the ROI is self-evident. Multi-location complexity creates more pain points and more value to capture.

---

## The Starving Crowd

Owners in this range are starving because:

1. **They're losing patients they paid to acquire.** 80% of first-visit patients never return. They know patients "disappear" but have no system to stop it.
2. **They've been burned by agencies.** They've paid $3K–$5K/month for social media posts that didn't move revenue. Skeptical but desperate.
3. **Multi-location complexity is killing them.** Two or three locations means fragmented scheduling, inconsistent follow-up, and no visibility across the practice.
4. **Competition is accelerating.** A new med spa opens every day. Corporate-backed groups are outspending them.
5. **Retention is the highest-leverage play they're NOT making.** They spend 80–100% of marketing budget on acquisition and 0% on retention.

They have the money. They have the pain. They don't have a solution. They're not browsing. They're bleeding.

## How to Pitch on the Diagnostic Call (SPICED Framework)

30 minutes, structured using WbD's SPICED diagnostic. Every call produces a scored profile — the standard handoff to implementation.

### Situation (0–3 min) — Closed-ended, prove you did your homework
"You have [X] locations in [market], using [scheduling platform]. Is that right?"

Confirm the basic facts. Show you understand her practice before she explains it.

### Pain (3–8 min) — Open-ended with peer context
"Other owners in your position say no-shows and inconsistent rebooking are their biggest revenue leaks. To what extent is that true for you?"

"What have you tried to fix it? What happened?"

Listen for the specific language she uses. That language goes into the pitch.

### Impact (8–15 min) — Quantify the leak
"How many active patients across locations? Average ticket? New patients per month?"

Calculate retention math live:
- "You see 400 patients/month at $536 average across 2 locations. That's $214K/month or $2.6M/year."
- "If half don't come back, you're losing ~$1.3M/year in potential recurring revenue."
- "Do you have automated follow-up after treatments? Rebooking sequences? No-show prevention?"
- The answer is almost always no.

### Critical Event (15–20 min) — Timeline and urgency
"Is there a time by which you need to see this working?"

"What happens if this doesn't get fixed in the next quarter?"

Listen for seasonal pressures (summer slowdown, competitor opening nearby, lease renewal).

### Decision (20–30 min) — Pitch and close
"Here's what I'd recommend. Let us run the Patient Revenue Recovery Audit. Fourteen days. We'll map every patient touchpoint, run the retention math on your actual data, benchmark you against the 5 practices closest to you, and deliver a 90-day roadmap with dollar amounts on every initiative."

"If the audit doesn't find at least 5x the fee in recoverable revenue, you don't pay."

"Most multi-location practices we look at are leaking $150K–$750K/year across missed visits, no-shows, dropped plans, and patients who never rebook. The audit shows you exactly where it is going."

If she's ready: "Want to get on the calendar for the kickoff? I have an opening next week."

If she has objections:
- **"I've tried agencies before"** — "This isn't a marketing engagement. We're not running your social media. We're looking at your patient data and finding where revenue is leaking. If the numbers aren't there, we'll tell you that."
- **"I don't have time"** — "The audit takes 90 minutes of your time total. We pull the data ourselves."
- **"How much?"** — "$4,800 flat for the audit across your first 3 locations. If we don't find at least 5x that in provable recoverable annual revenue, you don't pay."
- **"I need to think about it"** — "Totally fair. While you're thinking, every week of inaction is roughly $1,000–$2,500 in patients you paid to acquire who won't come back. Happy to follow up Thursday — does that work?"
