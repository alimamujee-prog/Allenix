## Guarantee Structure (Hormozi-Style)

Every offer has three layers of risk reversal:

1. **Performance guarantee:** Specific revenue recovery target. Miss it, work for free until it's hit.
2. **Cancel anytime:** Monthly contracts. No long lock-in for Quick Start and Growth System. (Transformation has 6-month minimum because real transformation takes time.)
3. **The "Allenix Promise":** "If at any point you feel like you're not getting 10x the value of what you're paying, tell us. We'll either fix it or end the engagement. No hard feelings."

This removes all three fears:
- "What if it doesn't work?" → Performance guarantee
- "What if I'm stuck in a contract?" → Cancel anytime
- "What if they stop caring after I sign?" → 10x value promise

## What Happens on the Assessment Call (Sales Process)

The assessment call is the conversion mechanism. Here's the structure:

### Minute 0-5: Connection
- "Tell me about your practice. How long have you been at it? What made you start?"
- Listen. Build rapport. Understand her specific situation.

### Minute 5-15: The Diagnosis
- "How many active patients do you have? What's your average ticket? How many new patients per month?"
- Calculate her retention math live:
  - "If you have 1,200 patients a year at $536/visit and 3 visits/year, that's ~$1.9M. If half don't come back, you're losing ~$960K in potential recurring revenue."
- "Do you have automated follow-up after treatments? Rebooking sequences? No-show prevention?"
- Usually the answer is no or "we try but it's inconsistent."

### Minute 15-20: The Pitch
- "Here's what we'd do: [Quick Start description]. We can have it running in 2 weeks. Based on practices similar to yours, this typically recovers $5K-$10K/month in missed rebookings."
- "If it doesn't pay for itself in 60 days, you don't pay the monthly fee until it does."
- "Want to see the specific plan for your practice?"

### Close
- Don't oversell. The math sells itself.
- The question is never "should I do this?" — it's "when can you start?"
- If she has objections, address them with the specific case studies
