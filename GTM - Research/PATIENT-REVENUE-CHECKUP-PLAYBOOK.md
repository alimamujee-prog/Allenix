# Patient Revenue Checkup Playbook

## Date
2026-04-23

---

## Purpose

The Patient Revenue Checkup is a 30-minute call that sells the Patient Revenue Recovery Audit.

It is not a free consulting call. It is not a pitch for a retainer. It is a live money-leak conversation that makes the paid Audit feel like the safest next step.

Primary close:

**"The next step is the Patient Revenue Recovery Audit. It is $4,800. If we do not find at least 5x that in provable recoverable annual revenue, you do not pay."**

---

## Call Flow

### 0-3 minutes: Confirm the frame

"I did a little homework before the call. Looks like you have [X] locations in [city/market], and you are using [Zenoti/PatientNow/Mindbody] for scheduling. Is that right?"

"The point of this call is simple. I want to see if there is enough revenue leaking from missed visits, no-shows, and patients who never rebook to justify a proper audit. If the math is not there, I will tell you."

### 3-8 minutes: Surface the pain

"When owners get past one location, follow-up usually gets inconsistent. One front desk is strong. Another is overloaded. Nobody can see the whole picture. Where does that show up for you?"

"What happens right now when a patient finishes a treatment and does not rebook?"

"How are no-shows handled across locations?"

"Do you know which location has the best rebooking rate?"

### 8-15 minutes: Do rough retention math live

Ask:
- "About how much annual revenue are you doing across locations?"
- "How many locations?"
- "Average ticket?"
- "Roughly how many new patients per month?"
- "What percent of first-time patients come back within 90 or 120 days?"
- "Which system runs scheduling and patient communication?"

Simple math:

```text
Annual revenue x estimated retention leak = annual leak
Annual leak / locations = leak by location
Annual leak / 12 = monthly recoverable opportunity
```

Talk track:

"If you are doing $7M across 3 locations and even 10 percent of revenue is recoverable through better rebooking, no-show prevention, and reactivation, that is $700K/year. I am not saying all of that is easy money. I am saying the leak is large enough to measure properly."

### 15-20 minutes: Show 3-5 likely leaks

Use their answers to name likely leak points:

1. One-and-done patients who never get a treatment-specific follow-up.
2. Patients who should be rebooked on a treatment cycle but leave without the next visit.
3. No-shows and late cancellations without automated save sequences.
4. Dormant patients who have not visited in 6+ months.
5. Missed calls or slow replies during peak hours.

"These are not marketing problems. These are revenue recovery problems."

### 20-26 minutes: Pitch the Audit

"Here is what I would recommend. Let us run the Patient Revenue Recovery Audit."

"In 14 days, we map where $150K-$750K/year is leaking across your locations and give you a 90-day plan to recover it using the tools you already pay for."

"You get three things:"

1. "A location-by-location Money Leak Map."
2. "A 90-day Retention Sprint roadmap with dollar values per play."
3. "A 1-page owner summary you can show a partner, lender, buyer, or leadership team."

"It is $4,800 flat for up to 3 locations. If we do not find at least 5x that in provable recoverable annual revenue, you do not pay."

### 26-30 minutes: Close

"Based on what you told me, I think the audit is worth doing. Do you want to start next week?"

If yes:

"Great. We will send the agreement and a short data-access checklist. It should take your team less than 30 minutes."

If not ready:

"Totally fair. What would you need to know before you would feel comfortable moving forward?"

## Objection Scripts

### "I have tried agencies before."

"That is exactly why this starts with your data, not a campaign. We are not selling posts or ads. We are looking at patients you already paid to acquire and finding where they are leaking out of the business. If the numbers are not there, we will tell you."

### "We already use Zenoti / PatientNow for that."

"You probably have the features. Most practices do. The problem is that features do not run themselves. We configure the follow-up, the timing, the segments, and the reporting so the system actually produces revenue."

### "My front desk handles follow-up."

"They probably handle more than anyone sees. The question is whether it is consistent across every provider, treatment, and location. The audit is not about blaming the front desk. It shows which parts should stop depending on memory."

### "I am too busy right now."

"That is usually the symptom. The audit takes one kickoff and read-only access. We do the digging. If the leak is real, you get a plan that removes work from your team instead of adding more."

### "Will patients think the messages are impersonal?"

"Bad automation feels impersonal. Treatment-specific follow-up feels like care. A Botox patient should not get the same message as a laser hair removal patient. The point is to make the follow-up feel more attentive, not less."

### "What about HIPAA?"

"We keep the messaging inside HIPAA-safe appointment and care-follow-up boundaries, and we use the tools your practice already trusts for patient communication. The audit also flags anything that should go through compliance review before launch."

### "I need to think about it."

"Of course. Usually that means one of three things: you are not sure the leak is real, you are not sure we can find it, or you are not sure your team can handle another project. Which one is it?"

### "Can you send me information?"

"I can. The only thing I do not want to do is send a generic deck that does not apply to your practice. What would be most useful: the audit deliverables, the data-access checklist, or an example of the money-leak math?"

## Founder-Led Learning Loop

For the first 10-15 calls, tag every call with:
- Platform: Zenoti, PatientNow, Mindbody, other.
- Locations.
- Revenue band.
- Stated pain.
- Biggest objection.
- Audit close result.
- Whether they will allow case-study rights.

After 10 calls, revise:
- The promise.
- The objection scripts.
- The landing page hero.
- The audit deliverable names.
