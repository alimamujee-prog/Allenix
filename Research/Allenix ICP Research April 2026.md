# Allenix ICP Persona Research Brief: $5M–$50M Founder-Led Companies in Houston & Texas

## Executive Summary

This research maps the emotional, functional, and decision-making landscape of founder-led and CEO-led companies in the $5M–$50M revenue band across Houston and Texas, with the goal of informing Allenix's offer design, positioning, messaging, and sales process for AI transformation services.

The most important finding: **adoption is nearly universal, but execution is broken.** 91% of middle-market companies say they are using generative AI, up from 77% the year before ([RSM 2025 Middle Market AI Survey](https://rsmus.com/newsroom/2025/middle-market-firms-rapidly-embracing-generative-ai-but-expertise-gaps-pose-risks-rsm-2025-ai-survey.html)). 76% of SMB CEOs report personally using generative AI ([Vistage Q4 2025 CEO Confidence Index](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/ceo-confidence-enters-2026-with-measured-optimism-q4-vistage-ceo-index/)). Yet only 12% of CEOs globally say AI has delivered both cost and revenue benefits, and 56% report no significant financial impact yet ([PwC 29th Global CEO Survey, January 2026](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2026/pwc-2026-global-ceo-survey.html)). MIT's State of AI in Business 2025 report found that only about 5% of custom enterprise AI tools reach production ([MIT NANDA](https://mlq.ai/media/quarterly_decks/v0.1_State_of_AI_in_Business_2025_Report.pdf)).

The result is a Texas-flavored market of founders who are exhausted, suspicious of consultants, sitting on messy data and legacy systems, and watching peers at YPO/Vistage dinners brag about AI wins they privately suspect are bullshit. The opportunity is for an operator-led, Houston-based AI firm that shows up with receipts in 30 days, not a 90-page slide deck.

## 1. BANT-Ranked Industry Vertical Analysis

Verticals were surfaced by mapping Houston/Texas density data (Greater Houston Partnership, Dallas Fed, SBA), private equity activity, and the presence of founder-led firms in the $5M–$50M band, then scored against BANT criteria. The top seven rank as follows.

### 1.1 Energy Services & Oilfield Services (Upstream, Midstream, Equipment, Specialty)
**BANT Score: 9 / 10 – Highest priority vertical**

- **Budget:** Excellent for established players; Houston has 600+ oil & gas firms and the energy services sector employed 633,938 people in July 2025 ([Energy Workforce & Technology Council](https://www.energyworkforce.org/july-jobs-report-energy-services-sector-remains-steady-as-labor-market-slows-further/)). Margins are volatile with WTI at ~$60/bbl in early 2026, down from $75/bbl a year prior, which is *tightening* not killing willingness to invest in cost-side AI ([Greater Houston Partnership 2026 Economic Forecast](https://wpb.houston.org/app/uploads/2026/01/Glance-January-2026-Final.pdf)).
- **Authority:** Highly concentrated. Founder-CEOs and private-equity-installed CEOs (EnCap, Post Oak, Sterling, Juniper Capital, Five Point) dominate the $5M–$50M band. Clear single decision-maker.
- **Need:** Urgent. One operator using AI-powered Integrated Operations Centers achieved a 3× increase in well surveillance efficiency, enabling one person to monitor 150+ wells ([OPX AI](https://www.opxai.com/the-future-of-oil-gas-operations-integrated-ai-driven/)). Early adopters of AI maintenance report 30–40% reductions in equipment failure ([OPX AI, same source](https://www.opxai.com/the-future-of-oil-gas-operations-integrated-ai-driven/)).
- **Timing:** Sharp buying window. 71% of energy employers say they cannot fill open roles ([Energy Capital HTX](https://energycapitalhtx.com/energy-workforce-houston)); the Dallas Fed says oil & gas layoffs "reflect a longer-term trend as companies in the sector move toward greater efficiency using fewer workers to produce similar volumes" ([CultureMap Houston / GHP](https://houston.culturemap.com/news/innovation/greater-houston-jobs-forecast-2026/)). Halliburton and SLB are both pivoting to AI/digital as drilling demand wanes ([BOE Report, Oct 2025](https://boereport.com/2025/10/27/oilfield-giants-pivot-to-booming-ai-infrastructure-as-drilling-demand-wanes/)), which shifts competitive pressure to mid-market services.

### 1.2 Specialty Trade Contractors & Home Services (HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical, Mechanical)
**BANT Score: 9 / 10**

- **Budget:** Strong and accelerating. PE firms have bought nearly 800 HVAC, plumbing and electrical companies since 2022 ([WSJ via American Investment Council](https://www.investmentcouncil.org/icymi-wsj-highlights-how-private-equity-transforms-plumbing-and-hvac-small-businesses-boosting-wages-and-growth/); [KTRH Houston](https://ktrh.iheart.com/featured/houston-texas-news/content/2024-10-17-private-equity-firms-like-investing-in-successful-plumbing-hvac-businesses/)). Blackstone paid ~18.5× EBITDA ($2.5B) for Champions Group in 2025 ([Profitability Partners](https://profitabilitypartners.io/private-equity-hvac/)).
- **Authority:** Founder or operating CEO, very clean decision path.
- **Need:** Extreme. Multi-location rollups need reporting infrastructure, dispatch AI, and integrated financials. The Chill Brothers (Houston residential HVAC, Forum Equity Partners) acquired Texas Central Air in 2025 ([PrivSource](https://www.privsource.com/acquisitions/hvac-mechanical/state/texas)); Southern Home Services added Nick's Plumbing & Air Conditioning in Houston as its fourth Texas location ([PR Newswire, Oct 2025](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/southern-home-services-expands-texas-footprint-with-acquisition-of-nicks-plumbing--air-conditioning-in-houston-302590464.html)).
- **Timing:** Acquisition activity and "integrate-to-exit" valuation pressure create a 12–18 month buying window. Houston's long cooling season ensures non-discretionary demand; HVAC grew on average 6% during recessions ([Profitability Partners](https://profitabilitypartners.io/private-equity-hvac/)).

### 1.3 Industrial & Commercial Construction / EPC Contractors
**BANT Score: 8 / 10**

- **Budget:** Large Houston commercial construction market; Comfort Systems USA (Houston-HQ MEP) did $6.0B in 2024 ([Sig.ai Construction Tech Rankings](https://geo.sig.ai/rankings/real-estate-construction)). Mid-market players in the $5M–$50M range frequently run on Sage 300, Viewpoint, Acumatica, or QuickBooks.
- **Authority:** Founder-CEO, usually first-generation. Often family-owned.
- **Need:** Margin protection and job-cost visibility. Per Manufacturing Tomorrow, mid-size contractors lack real-time margin visibility; material overruns surface weeks after procurement, and rework gets buried in production reports ([Manufacturing Tomorrow, Feb 2026](https://www.manufacturingtomorrow.com/story/2026/02/the-margin-blind-spot-facing-advanced-manufacturers/26968/)).
- **Timing:** Construction will add jobs in Houston in 2026 even as oil & gas contracts ([GHP 2026 Economic Outlook](https://www.houston.org/news/greater-houston-partnership-forecasts-over-30000-new-jobs-in-2026/)). ERP migrations (Sage 300 to Acumatica, QuickBooks to Acumatica Construction Edition) are active and create a natural "while we're in there, let's add AI" entry point.

### 1.4 Distribution, Wholesale & Industrial Supply
**BANT Score: 8 / 10**

- **Budget:** Houston is an export/logistics hub with deep-water ports and 6,400+ manufacturers producing $80B+ annually ([HOUSTEX / MTSeries](https://southwest.mtseries.com/news/houston-manufacturing-drives-growth-in-texas/)). Wholesale Electric Supply Company of Houston does ~$42.9M across 33 locations ([ZoomInfo](https://www.zoominfo.com/c/wholesale-electric-supply-company-of-houston-inc/124076072)) — squarely in cohort B.
- **Authority:** Founder or second-generation CEO. Often with a controller but no CIO.
- **Need:** Inventory AI, demand forecasting, pricing, customer segmentation. Verusen and similar supply-chain AI platforms are finding easy wins in mid-market distribution ([Cherry Bekaert panel](https://www.cbh.com/insights/articles/how-ai-is-transforming-manufacturing-mid-market-companies/)).
- **Timing:** Tariff volatility (2025–2026) and labor cost pressure are forcing operators to rethink pricing and margins. 70% of CEOs in Q3/Q4 2025 cited tariffs as a top challenge ([Vistage WSJ Small Business Survey](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/small-business-confidence-fades-uncertainty-evolves-wsj-survey/)).

### 1.5 Healthcare Services (Physician Practice Management, Behavioral Health, Home Health, Specialty Clinics)
**BANT Score: 7.5 / 10**

- **Budget:** Texas has the most PE-owned hospitals of any state (108) ([Private Equity Stakeholder Project](https://pestakeholder.org/private-equity-hospital-tracker/)). Healthcare services saw 172 PE deals in a single recent quarter ([SSACPA](https://www.ssacpa.com/private-equity-healthcare-ruling/)). Houston healthcare will add 14,000 jobs in 2026, ~45% of all regional net job growth ([GHP via InnovationMap](https://houston.innovationmap.com/ghp-2026-jobs-forecast-2674402418.html)).
- **Authority:** Mixed. Founder-physician CEOs still common in $5M–$15M; PE-installed CEOs with medical directors at $15M+. PE presence can either accelerate or complicate the sale cycle.
- **Need:** Revenue cycle management, documentation, coding, intake, scheduling. AI accounting market hit $10.87B in 2026; over 80% of routine tax prep can be automated ([MindStudio Professional Services](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/professional-services)).
- **Timing:** Post-FTC-vs-USAP (Texas court dismissed the FTC case against Welsh Carson) ([Middle Market Growth](https://middlemarketgrowth.org/healthcare-private-equity-antitrust-regulation/)), PE deal activity is accelerating in Texas healthcare; every deal creates integration pain.

### 1.6 Professional Services & Engineering Firms
**BANT Score: 7 / 10**

- **Budget:** Professional and technical services is one of Houston's top job-growth sectors for 2026 ([GHP 2026 Economic Outlook](https://www.houston.org/news/greater-houston-partnership-forecasts-over-30000-new-jobs-in-2026/)). Founder-partners in $5M–$30M engineering, consulting, surveying, and accounting firms have discretionary budget.
- **Authority:** Founder-managing partner; sometimes a partnership with dominant founder.
- **Need:** Document automation, proposal generation, reporting. McKinsey found professional services AI implementation rates jumped from 33% in 2023 to 71% in 2024 ([MindStudio](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/professional-services)).
- **Timing:** Competitive pressure from Big 4 AI offerings and boutique operator shops. 79% of legal professionals now incorporate AI tools ([MindStudio](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/professional-services)).

### 1.7 Logistics, Trucking & Multi-Location Field Service
**BANT Score: 7 / 10**

- **Budget:** Texas freight and trucking is enormous; Houston has two international airports and four deep-water seaports ([TexasRealEstateSource / Houston Economy](https://www.texasrealestatesource.com/blog/houston-economy/)).
- **Authority:** Founder-CEO, frequently owner-operator origin story.
- **Need:** Dispatch AI, driver mobile, invoice automation, IFTA compliance, predictive maintenance. AI-native TMS products (Rose Rocket, Alvys, Vektor, LoadStop, Toro) are actively being sold into $5M–$50M carriers ([FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/perspectives/ideaxchange/blog/55329822/how-tms-technology-is-transforming-truckingfrom-filing-cabinets-to-ai-co-pilots)).
- **Timing:** Diesel price volatility, driver shortage, insurance cost spikes are pushing operators to rethink automation. "Management must focus on what they can control" ([BeyondTrucks CEO via FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/perspectives/ideaxchange/blog/55329822/how-tms-technology-is-transforming-truckingfrom-filing-cabinets-to-ai-co-pilots)).

**Verticals that deliberately rank below the top 7:** Retail/restaurant SMB (thin margins, franchisor constraints), non-profits (budget), pure technology startups (they build their own AI), and pure oil & gas upstream E&P (dominated by large public companies or PE-installed teams that go directly to McKinsey or Accenture).

## 2. Layer-by-Layer Findings with Cohort Comparison

### Layer 1: Current State of Awareness & Sentiment

**What they know vs. what they perform knowing.** 76% of SMB CEOs report personally using generative AI, an increase of 10 points in nine months ([Vistage "CEO AI Leadership Blind Spot"](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-operations/business-technology/ceo-ai-leadership/)). But Vistage explicitly calls out a gap: "casual usage is being mistaken for digital engagement, and the gap between 'AI slop' and meaningful use is widening." Most CEOs use ChatGPT as a smarter search engine and call it "doing AI."

**Where information comes from:**
- **Cohort A ($5M–$15M):** YouTube, LinkedIn posts, podcasts (My First Million, All-In, Acquired), vendor webinars, and whichever peer at their Vistage small-business group ($1M–$20M) sounded most confident last month. Vistage's small-business segment surveys CEOs with annual revenues of $1M–$20M ([Vistage / WSJ Small Business Survey](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/20250522-small-business-confidence-rises-wsj-survey/)).
- **Cohort B ($15M–$50M):** Vistage Chief Executive groups (25+ employees, $10M+ revenue), YPO forums, their CFO/controller, their Big 4 audit partner, and increasingly their PE sponsor if one exists. YPO explicitly frames AI as "the next great crossroad for CEOs" ([YPO](https://www.ypo.org/2025/09/building-your-ai-roadmap/)).

**Sentiment by cohort:**

| Dimension | Cohort A ($5M–$15M) | Cohort B ($15M–$50M) |
|---|---|---|
| Dominant emotion | Overwhelmed / paralyzed | Urgent / caught between two fires |
| Excitement | Real, but private | Performative, for peers/board |
| Skepticism | "I've been burned before" | "Big 4 pitched us and it was garbage" |
| Paralysis trigger | Nobody to delegate to | Nobody the CEO *trusts* to delegate to |
| Confidence | Low; hides it | Medium; over-performs certainty |

**Convergence:** Both cohorts show AI fatigue; 53% of SMB CEOs are NOT training their workforce on AI despite the pressure ([Vistage Q1 2025 data](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-operations/business-technology/20250807-ai-adoption-in-business/)). Only 5% of organizations globally are getting material value from AI at scale (BCG via Vistage) ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-operations/business-technology/enterprise-ai-adoption-roi/)).

**Divergence by vertical:** Energy services CEOs are more data-fluent and less afraid of technology than home services or distribution founders; professional and business services report the *highest* AI use in Texas, while leisure, hospitality, and manufacturing report the lowest ([Dallas Fed TBOS](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2024/0625)).

**What They Actually Say (Layer 1):**
- "Everyone's telling me to do AI but nobody can tell me where to start."
- "I use ChatGPT to write emails. Does that count?"
- "My kid showed me this thing called Claude. It's wild."
- "Half the stuff on LinkedIn about AI is bullshit. The other half I don't understand."
- "I went to a Vistage meeting and a guy said he saved $400K with AI. I nodded. I have no idea if that's real."
- "We have a guy on the team who 'does AI.' I don't actually know what he does."
- "I'm tired of hearing about it. I want someone to just do it."
- "If I hear 'agentic' one more time I'm going to throw my laptop."
- "I'm sure we're behind. I just don't know how far behind."
- *(Inferred, patterned after Vistage member quote)* "I use it daily as my thought partner… to poke holes in some of my strategy" — Eddie Russnow, MAC Products ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/ceo-confidence-enters-2026-with-measured-optimism-q4-vistage-ceo-index/)).

### Layer 2: Emotional Landscape

**What keeps them up at night.** The Vistage May 2025 Small Business survey found 94% of leaders reporting symptoms of burnout over the prior year, and 32% report regular exhaustion ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/20250522-small-business-confidence-rises-wsj-survey/)). One respondent captured it: "Between turnover and staffing and the persistent gap between expectations, capacity is draining. We're constantly triaging priorities."

**Specific fears, by cohort:**

| Fear | Cohort A | Cohort B |
|---|---|---|
| Looking stupid in front of peers | High | High — this is the dominant fear at YPO/Vistage |
| Getting left behind | Medium (they think they have time) | Extreme (they think they have a quarter) |
| Wasting money | High (it's *their* money) | High (it's the board's / PE's money) |
| Losing control | Extreme — every decision routes to founder | High — founder fears what COO will do unsupervised |
| Being replaced by AI | Low (personally); medium for team | Medium — they see BCG saying "the CEO's job depends on AI" ([BCG](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2026/as-ai-investments-surge-ceos-take-the-lead)) |
| Breaking what works | Extreme | Extreme |

BCG's 2026 AI Radar found that 50% of CEOs surveyed believe their job stability depends on successfully integrating AI in 2026, and among non-CEOs, more than half believe the CEO or board should resign if the company loses market share to competitors due to an inadequate AI strategy ([World Economic Forum / BCG](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/01/ceos-are-all-in-on-ai-but-anxieties-remain/)). That's the quiet terror sitting underneath every "I'm fine" at a Vistage meeting.

**Who makes them feel behind:**
- Cohort A: The friend from Vistage who "built an AI bot that does all his invoices now." The cousin at a tech company. Their 24-year-old nephew.
- Cohort B: A competitor that got acquired at 12× EBITDA instead of 7× because the PE buyer liked their "tech stack." A board member who keeps asking "what's our AI story." Their Big 4 auditor who says "we've seen clients cut controllers with this."

**Emotional cost of inaction vs. acting and failing:**
- **Inaction cost:** Reputation at peer table; loss of perceived leadership; slow enterprise-value erosion (a dealmaker quoted in William & Wall: investors increasingly view AI capability as a value driver in Texas M&A ([William & Wall](https://www.williamandwall.com/blog/private-equitys-business-wishlist-texas-and-the-lone-star-advantage))).
- **Acting and failing cost:** Direct cash loss ($50K–$500K typical pilot range), team mutiny, founder looks stupid, and ops disruption during a single bad quarter can cost a year of growth. EY found that fewer than 1% of executives report *significant* ROI from AI investments ([Forbes AI Study 2025 via Mavvrik](https://www.mavvrik.ai/blog/forbes-ai-study-2025/)).

**What They Actually Say (Layer 2):**
- "I just don't want to look stupid in front of my Vistage group."
- "I don't trust anyone to do this right, and I don't have the time to do it myself."
- "My competitor just sold for a 12 multiple. Their website says 'AI-powered.' I know their business — it's bullshit. But the buyer believed it."
- "What if I spend $200K on this and my ops manager quits because she's confused?"
- "I built this company for 18 years. I'm not going to blow it up because Sam Altman is on a podcast."
- "My wife is tired of hearing about AI. My COO is tired of hearing about AI. I'm tired of hearing about AI. But I can't stop."
- "Everyone under 30 acts like this is obvious and everyone over 50 acts like it's fine. Both of them are wrong."
- "I feel like I'm supposed to have an answer when the board asks. I don't."
- "I'm not scared of AI. I'm scared of wasting another quarter."
- "I already wasted $60K on a CRM implementation two years ago. I'm not doing that again."

### Layer 3: Functional Reality

**What they've actually tried.** The distribution across the middle market looks like this: 91% have *used* generative AI in some form, 79% claim a defined AI strategy, but only 25% say AI is fully integrated into core operations ([RSM 2025 Middle Market AI Survey](https://rsmus.com/newsroom/2025/middle-market-firms-rapidly-embracing-generative-ai-but-expertise-gaps-pose-risks-rsm-2025-ai-survey.html)). The SBA Business Trends and Outlook Survey, which uses stricter definitions, shows far lower "production" rates ([USM Systems summary](https://usmsystems.com/small-business-ai-adoption-statistics/)).

**Actual attempts, Cohort A:**
- ChatGPT Plus subscriptions for the founder and 2–3 senior people
- A website chatbot bought from a SaaS vendor for $5K–$15K/year that nobody uses after 30 days ([Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/science-technology/will-a-chatbot-really-save-your-company-money/368552))
- A content tool (Jasper, Copy.ai) for marketing
- One attempt at a custom automation built by a freelancer who left
- Zapier / Make automations built by an ops person

**Actual attempts, Cohort B:**
- Microsoft Copilot rollout (41% of organizations have a generative AI license or subscription as of Q1 2025) ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-operations/business-technology/20250807-ai-adoption-in-business/))
- A Big 4 or boutique consulting pilot that produced a "beautiful deck" and stalled (EY, KPMG, Accenture cited in [National CIO Review](https://nationalcioreview.com/articles-insights/extra-bytes/consulting-in-crisis-ais-boom-leaves-big-firms-struggling-to-keep-up/))
- RPA / workflow automation via UiPath, Automation Anywhere, or a partner
- A Power BI dashboard project that half-works
- An internal "innovation committee" that hasn't shipped anything

**What worked / what broke.** MIT NANDA's State of AI in Business 2025 found that generic LLM chatbots show ~83% pilot-to-implementation rates, but *custom* enterprise systems only reach ~5% production ([MIT NANDA](https://mlq.ai/media/quarterly_decks/v0.1_State_of_AI_in_Business_2025_Report.pdf)). The pattern: easy wins succeed; workflow-critical tools fail for lack of memory, customization, and learning loops. One mid-market manufacturing COO quoted in that report described the gap bluntly: chatbots succeed because they're flexible; AI fails in critical workflows because it can't adapt.

**Who they hired.** AmeriSave's CIO captured the consulting experience in a representative quote: "They overpromised… we discovered that they really also had no idea how to do these things. They were just as good or as bad as what we would have been able to do in house." ([Crepuscular Circus analysis](https://crepuscular-circus.ghost.io/ai-puts-consultancies-between-a-rock-and-a-hard-place/)). A separate case describes a company that paid $2M for a Big 4 AI strategy where the pilot worked only in a controlled environment and couldn't survive real data; the consultants had moved on to the next client by the time it was time to implement ([DAS Advanced Systems](https://dasadvancedsystems.com/blog/why-big-4-consulting-firms-are-failing-mid-size-companies-with-ai/)).

**Spend and outcome.** RSM's data breaks down the top barriers: 39% cite lack of in-house expertise, 34% lack a clear AI strategy, 32% cite data quality issues, and 41% ran into data quality problems during rollout ([RSM](https://rsmus.com/newsroom/2025/middle-market-firms-rapidly-embracing-generative-ai-but-expertise-gaps-pose-risks-rsm-2025-ai-survey.html)). 92% of organizations that deployed generative AI encountered challenges during rollout.

**Tech stack reality:**

| Stack element | Cohort A | Cohort B |
|---|---|---|
| Accounting | QuickBooks Desktop/Online, sometimes Sage 50 | NetSuite, Sage Intacct, Acumatica, Dynamics 365 BC, sometimes mid-tier ERP |
| CRM | None, or HubSpot Starter, sometimes Salesforce Essentials | Salesforce Professional/Enterprise, HubSpot Pro, industry-specific (ServiceTitan for trades) |
| Data | Spreadsheets. Everywhere. On someone's desktop | Power BI or Tableau dashboards that half-work; some data in warehouses |
| AI-ready? | No. Data is fragmented, un-labeled, and manual. | Partially. Some systems integrated; most reporting still manual. |
| IT | A break-fix MSP + the founder's nephew | A one-person IT director + an MSP + a Big 4 audit relationship |

From NTT Data and CIO.com: at the enterprise level, 50% of organizations say the need to modernize applications and data platforms is holding them back from cloud / AI innovation ([IT Brew](https://www.itbrew.com/stories/2026/03/30/how-to-get-your-legacy-data-ai-ready-and-fast)). For mid-market, 45% of companies admit data is not ready to back AI initiatives, with 19% saying it's the number-one blocker ([Slingshot via Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/science-technology/4-steps-companies-must-take-to-get-their-data-ready-for-ai/482182)).

**What They Actually Say (Layer 3):**
- "We don't even have clean data. How am I supposed to do AI?"
- "I spent $80K on a chatbot and nobody uses it."
- "My ops manager has three spreadsheets that run this company. Nobody else understands them. She's 58."
- "I hired a guy off Upwork. He built something that sort of worked. Then he ghosted."
- "The Big 4 came in with a 90-slide deck. I paid $200K for a PDF."
- "We have QuickBooks, a field service app, and a 14-year-old website. The 'integration' is my controller copy-pasting on Fridays."
- "Copilot is fine, I guess. Nobody really uses it."
- "We bought an RPA tool. The IT guy who set it up quit. Now it breaks every three weeks and nobody knows how to fix it."
- "I have a data warehouse. I have no idea if there's data in it."
- "Our BI dashboard is wrong. Everyone knows it's wrong. Nobody has time to fix it."

### Layer 4: Decision Architecture

**Who do they call first?**

- **Cohort A:** Their accountant, then a peer from Vistage, then a vendor who cold-called them last month. Often nobody — they stew for 90 days and then google "AI for [industry]" at 11 PM.
- **Cohort B:** Their COO or CFO first, then a board member or PE sponsor, then a peer from YPO or Vistage Chief Executive, then an advisor (fractional CIO, lawyer, accountant), *then* a vendor.

Vistage member data and Stanford Graduate School of Business research both note that almost 75% of CEOs do not receive outside leadership advice ([Vistage peer group research](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-leadership/20230215-6-reasons-peer-advisory-group/)). Mid-market CFOs specifically are telling the CFO Alliance: "Please don't send me the Big Four, the next-tier firms, or any group owned by private equity. That eliminates almost three-quarters of the market." ([CFO.com, Nov 2025](https://www.cfo.com/news/mid-market-cfos-turn-to-readiness-retention-and-realism-for-2026-nick-araco-cfo-alliance-diligent-/803085/)). The distrust of Big 4 + PE-owned advisors is real and quantifiable.

**Influencers:**

| Influencer | Cohort A weight | Cohort B weight |
|---|---|---|
| Spouse | High | Medium |
| Vistage / YPO peer | Very high | Extreme |
| Accountant / Fractional CFO | High | High |
| COO / VP Ops | N/A | Very high |
| Board / PE sponsor | None | Very high |
| Long-tenured employee ("she's been here 15 years") | Very high | High — "culture carrier" veto |
| Consultant | Low (distrust) | Medium (but guarded) |

**Triggers to action:**
1. **Peer pressure moment** — "My Vistage chair said something that haunted me" or "A guy in my group just sold his business for 2× what I thought it was worth."
2. **Talent crisis** — "My controller just gave notice and I can't replace her." Vistage research consistently shows staffing and capacity as the top challenges for SMBs ([Vistage May 2025 survey](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/20250522-small-business-confidence-rises-wsj-survey/)).
3. **Growth milestone** — "We just hit $25M and I feel it all falling apart."
4. **Acquisition event** — either selling (prep) or buying (integration). Texas has the most PE-owned hospitals in the country and a booming roll-up market in HVAC, healthcare services, and industrial services ([PrivSource](https://www.privsource.com/acquisitions/healthcare-services/state/texas)).
5. **ERP migration** — "We're moving off QuickBooks and the implementer said we should think about AI while we're at it."
6. **Regulatory shift** — Texas Responsible AI Governance Act (TRAIGA / HB 149) takes effect January 1, 2026, requiring disclosure when AI interacts with customers and creating compliance work for vendors and deployers ([Latham & Watkins](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/texas-signs-responsible-ai-governance-act-into-law); [Fluid IT](https://www.fluiditservices.com/blog/the-2026-texas-ai-readiness-guide-for-smbs)).
7. **Tariff / margin squeeze** — 2025-2026 tariff whiplash has pushed SMBs toward automation, with one Vistage respondent saying: "We are limiting new hiring for full-time roles and are instead looking into AI and automation to streamline our workflows." ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/20250522-small-business-confidence-rises-wsj-survey/)).

**What moves them from "someday" to "this quarter":**
- A peer referral from someone they respect.
- A credible, numbered promise ("in 90 days we will save you X hours of controller time and deliver one live AI workflow").
- A framework that lets them not have to build a team to manage it.
- A cost that's framed as "less than one hire" ($100K–$300K/year).

**Buying process:**
- **Cohort A:** 1–2 meetings, mostly with the founder. Decision in 2–4 weeks. Emotional, reference-driven, often a handshake.
- **Cohort B:** 3–6 meetings, usually with founder + COO/CFO + possibly a board observer. Decision in 6–12 weeks. MSA and SOW. References + case studies heavily weighted.

- "I'd buy it tomorrow if Rick told me he used them and it worked."

### Layer 5: Dream State & Jobs to Be Done

**If AI worked perfectly, what does the company look like in 12–18 months?**

Vistage's investment signaling suggests the answer: CEOs in Q4 2025 reported that AI investments are concentrated on "tools, training, and workflow support that improve productivity and extend the capacity of the existing workforce" ([Vistage Q4 2025](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-financials/economic-trends/ceo-confidence-enters-2026-with-measured-optimism-q4-vistage-ceo-index/)). Translation: they want more output from the same team, not a headcount bloodbath.

KeyBank's Middle Market Sentiment Report (2025) found mid-market companies expect the following benefits: 71% improved operational efficiencies/productivity, 60% improved decision-making/management of company ([KeyBank](https://www.key.com/businesses-institutions/business-expertise/articles/ai-trends-in-2025.html)).

**Dream state, Cohort A ($5M–$15M):**
- The founder takes a real vacation — the first in four years.
- The Friday afternoon "why isn't this invoice out yet" call doesn't happen anymore.
- They can add a $2M account without hiring two new people.
- Monthly financials are on their phone by the 5th, not the 20th.
- Customer quotes go out in 30 minutes, not 2 days.
- They stop being the human CRM.

**Dream state, Cohort B ($15M–$50M):**
- Multi-entity or multi-location P&L rolls up automatically.
- They can evaluate an acquisition without a 3-week diligence sprint.
- The COO has real dashboards, not 11-tab Excels from the controller.
- They can tell the board/PE "here's our AI scorecard" and mean it.
- Enterprise value goes up 1–3 turns of EBITDA at exit because the buyer sees integrated tech.
- They can walk into a YPO forum and not be the one lying about AI.

**Top 3 Jobs to Be Done (combined, ranked):**
1. **"Give me back my calendar and my brain."** Remove the founder from being the bottleneck on approvals, reporting, and reviews. Fractional COO / On Call COO data shows founder-controlled QA is the single most common scaling breakdown in $1M–$20M founder-led companies ([On Call COO](https://www.melissafranks.com/optin-blog/how-to-get-out-of-the-day-to-day-operations-of-your-business)).
2. **"Make my numbers trustworthy and fast."** Monthly close from 20 days to 5. Real-time margin by job/customer/SKU. One source of truth. Middle-market CFOs report reducing monthly close from two weeks to three or four days with automation ([Cherry Bekaert](https://www.cbh.com/insights/articles/top-3-challenges-facing-middle-market-cfos-in-2025/)).
3. **"Let me scale revenue without scaling headcount linearly."** Stop the "we grew 10% so we need 3 new coordinators" cycle. RSM data: 50% saved time on IT projects, 45% saved time in data analytics, 39% saved time in customer service ([RSM](https://rsmus.com/newsroom/2025/middle-market-firms-rapidly-embracing-generative-ai-but-expertise-gaps-pose-risks-rsm-2025-ai-survey.html)).

**The financial outcome they care most about:**
- **Cohort A:** *Time back.* Then margin expansion. Then revenue growth. Enterprise value is abstract to them.
- **Cohort B:** *Enterprise value (exit multiple)*, then margin expansion, then time back. Revenue growth is often already handled; efficiency is the unlock. PE-aware CEOs know that companies with mature tech stacks and integrated data get premium multiples in Texas M&A ([William & Wall](https://www.williamandwall.com/blog/private-equitys-business-wishlist-texas-and-the-lone-star-advantage)).

**"Winning" in their language, not tech language:**
- "I got my Fridays back."
- "We close the books on the 3rd now."
- "I grew $8M and we only added three people."
- "Our PE guy stopped asking about AI. He just nodded."
- "My COO stopped sending me panic texts on Sunday nights."
- "We hit plan without me losing my mind."
- "I can finally see which jobs make money in real time."

**What They Actually Say (Layer 5):**

### Layer 6: Objections, Barriers & Trust

**What "consulting" means to them.** It's almost universally negative in this segment. A survey of consulting client sentiment post-AI boom found clients "being sold theoretical futures, not present-day solutions," with consultants building prototypes that "lacked the depth or expertise to scale" ([National CIO Review](https://nationalcioreview.com/articles-insights/extra-bytes/consulting-in-crisis-ais-boom-leaves-big-firms-struggling-to-keep-up/)). CFO.com captured it in a direct quote from mid-market CFOs: "Please don't send me the Big Four… or any group owned by private equity." ([CFO.com](https://www.cfo.com/news/mid-market-cfos-turn-to-readiness-retention-and-realism-for-2026-nick-araco-cfo-alliance-diligent-/803085/)).

To a Houston founder-CEO, "consultant" means:
- A $350/hour MBA who has never run payroll.
- A deck-maker.
- Someone who disappears when it's time to implement.
- A billable-hour roach motel.
- Dishonest about what they actually know.

**Who they have been burned by (in order):**
1. **Big 4 / Tier 1 strategy firms** — Sold a vision, delivered a deck, billed for 18 months.
2. **Offshore dev shops** — Built something that kind of works, then nobody can maintain it. This is a repeated pattern in founder accounts ([eCommerce Badassery](https://ecommercebadassery.com/why-you-cant-trust-consultants/)).
3. **Big SaaS vendors** — Oversold the product, undersold the implementation, and the rep stopped returning calls after close.
4. **Freelancers / Upwork devs** — Solid for a project, then unavailable.
5. **Their MSP** — Great at "keep the email running," useless at AI strategy.
6. **A friend's nephew** — Enthusiastic, no delivery discipline.

**What a firm has to prove in the first 30 days:**
- **Shipped something live in production.** Not a pilot. Not a "proof of concept." A working thing.
- **A weekly scorecard** with hours saved / dollars moved / errors prevented.
- **Direct access to a senior operator**, not a rotating bench of juniors.
- **No surprise invoices.** One price, known scope.
- **Usable by the controller / ops manager without re-training.** If the 14-year-tenured ops manager can't use it, it's dead on arrival.

**Top objections, in order:**
1. **"I'm not ready. My data is a mess."** — Supported by multiple studies: 32–45% of mid-market and SMBs say data isn't AI-ready ([RSM](https://rsmus.com/newsroom/2025/middle-market-firms-rapidly-embracing-generative-ai-but-expertise-gaps-pose-risks-rsm-2025-ai-survey.html); [Slingshot via Entrepreneur](https://www.entrepreneur.com/science-technology/4-steps-companies-must-take-to-get-their-data-ready-for-ai/482182)).

Vistage data: only 47% of CEOs are training their workforce on AI ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-operations/business-technology/20250807-ai-adoption-in-business/)).
4. **"I've already been burned."** — Vendor / consultant trust is at a low ([National CIO Review](https://nationalcioreview.com/articles-insights/extra-bytes/consulting-in-crisis-ais-boom-leaves-big-firms-struggling-to-keep-up/)).

## 3. Composite Persona Narratives

### Cohort A: "Mark" — Founder-CEO, $9M Specialty Mechanical Contractor, Pearland

Mark is 54. He started the company out of his garage in 2009 after getting tired of working for a bigger contractor. Today he has 38 employees, $9M in revenue, and a location in Pearland. His wife does bookkeeping part-time until the company can "afford a real controller." QuickBooks Online, a field service app called ServiceTitan he hated paying for but now can't live without, a CRM that he bought during a conference and never really used, and a website that a nephew of a friend built in 2019.

His Friday afternoon goes like this: he gets in the truck at 6:45 AM, hits two job sites, ends up on the phone with a customer who's angry about an invoice his daughter-in-law sent out wrong, signs a $180K proposal in the parking lot of Shipley Do-Nuts, argues with his foreman about a missing part, gets back to the office at 5 PM, reviews the weekly numbers his wife put together in Excel (they're wrong, but within 5%), and then spends from 8 to 11 PM at his kitchen table trying to figure out why job #4412 lost money. He falls asleep on the couch still holding his phone.

He joined Vistage two years ago. His favorite member is a guy named Rick who runs a similar-sized commercial HVAC outfit. Rick said at the last meeting that he "put in some AI" and his office manager is doing the work of two people now. Mark went home that night and googled "AI for HVAC contractor" for an hour. He didn't click anything. The next morning he forgot about it. Three weeks later his controller — actually his wife — said something about needing help. That's the moment.

He distrusts consultants. He got burned in 2017 by a "process improvement firm" that charged him $45K for a binder he never opened. He will not sign an MSA. He will sign a one-page agreement if the person across the table has callused hands, is from Houston, and can tell him what they will actually deliver in 30 days. He has $75K in the bank for "technology stuff" he hasn't spent. He'd spend $150K if he believed it. He'd spend $250K if his wife said yes and Rick said yes.

What he wants, but would never say this way: **he wants to not be the smartest person in his own company.**

### Cohort B: "Angela" — CEO, $34M Industrial Services Platform, Energy Corridor

Angela is 48. She bought out her dad's share of the business in 2019 and has since bolted on two smaller acquisitions with help from a lower-middle-market PE sponsor based in Dallas. The company now has 210 employees across three Texas locations, runs on NetSuite (post-migration from Sage 100 two years ago, still painful), has a COO named Dave who's been there 22 years, a controller who's good but overwhelmed, a VP of Ops, and a "director of data and systems" she hired six months ago who is technically fine but isn't strategic.

She sits on the board of a local nonprofit, is in a YPO forum (and a Vistage Chief Executive group for her CFO), and flies to a PE-sponsor portfolio CEO dinner once a quarter. At that dinner, every single CEO is performing confidence about AI. Angela performs too. Internally, she is terrified that she doesn't actually know what's real and what's bullshit.

Her PE sponsor asked her on the last board call: "What's the AI roadmap for the combined entity?" She had a three-slide answer that she now thinks was garbage. Her Big 4 auditor offered to "run a readiness assessment" for $165K. She said she'd think about it. She didn't. She got a pitch from a Tier 1 strategy firm for a "90-day AI transformation diagnostic" at $420K. She laughed out loud and closed the email.

Dave, the 22-year COO, thinks AI is a waste of time. He says this in meetings. She doesn't disagree out loud but she knows he's wrong. She also knows that if she buys something that disrupts Dave, Dave will quietly sabotage it and she'll be blamed at the board. Her second-acquisition entity still doesn't roll up cleanly to the master P&L; her monthly close is 17 days; her revenue-per-FTE has flat-lined for two years; and her PE sponsor has started using the phrase "enterprise value creation levers" more often than she'd like.

She has an exit target — three years, ideally at 8×+ EBITDA. She knows from William & Wall, from her YPO peers, and from her PE sponsor that the next buyer will pay a premium for an AI/data story. She also knows two of her competitors got acquired in 2024 at multiples that shocked her.

What she wants: **a real partner. Not a consultant. Not a vendor. Someone who can walk into a room with Dave and survive the first 20 minutes, ship something live before the next board meeting, and not embarrass her in front of her PE sponsor.** She'd authorize $300K this quarter for the right firm. She'd authorize $1M over 18 months if the first 90 days went well.

## 4. Key Implications for Allenix

### 4.1 Positioning: "Houston Operators for Houston Operators"

The single highest-value positioning signal for this ICP is **operator + Houston + outcomes**. Not "AI consultancy." Not "digital transformation." Not "boutique strategy." Every published mid-market CEO study — from Vistage to RSM to EY — reinforces that this cohort distrusts consultants and gravitates to peer / operator voices. The PwC 29th CEO Survey found only 12% of CEOs say AI has delivered both cost and revenue benefits ([PwC](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2026/pwc-2026-global-ceo-survey.html)) — Allenix's positioning should explicitly acknowledge that reality rather than pretend.

**Recommended tagline direction:**
- "AI that ships in 30 days — or you don't pay."
- "We run ops. We don't write decks."
- "Houston-built AI for Houston founders."

Avoid: "transformation," "journey," "roadmap," "partner in your innovation," "unlock," "synergy," "digital future."

### 4.2 Offer Design: The 30-Day Live Deliverable

Build the headline offer around a **30-day live deliverable** at a named, single price. The research is unambiguous that mid-market CEOs will buy "one thing that ships in 30 days" far more readily than "a 90-day roadmap." MIT NANDA found top-performing mid-market companies moved from pilot to implementation in 90 days vs. enterprises that take a year ([MIT NANDA](https://mlq.ai/media/quarterly_decks/v0.1_State_of_AI_in_Business_2025_Report.pdf)).

**Suggested offer structure:**
- **Tier 1 — "AI Ground Zero" (Cohort A, $5M–$15M):** $45K–$75K, 30 days, one live workflow shipped (quoting bot, AR aging automation, inbox summarizer, proposal generator). Month-to-month managed services after.
- **Tier 2 — "AI Operator" (Cohort B lower half, $15M–$30M):** $125K–$175K, 60 days, 2-3 live workflows + reporting layer. Quarterly scorecard.
- **Tier 3 — "AI Integration & Reporting" (Cohort B upper half, $30M–$50M):** $250K–$400K, 90 days, data unification + multi-entity reporting + 3 live AI workflows. Retainer thereafter.

All tiers: **one flat price, one senior operator on the account, one scorecard, zero MSA gymnastics.**

### 4.3 Vertical Focus Sequence

Given BANT rankings and Allenix's Houston base, the recommended 2025-2026 go-to-market sequence:

1. **Energy services & oilfield services** — highest BANT fit, Houston density, and cost pressure from WTI decline is a now-catalyst.
2. **Specialty trade / home services** — PE rollup tailwind, founder-friendly, "help me get ready to sell" framing converts.
3. **Industrial construction / EPC** — ERP migrations are the Trojan horse.
4. **Distribution / wholesale** — tariff pressure is a live catalyst.
5. **Healthcare services** — longer sales cycle but PE money is there.
6. **Professional services / engineering** — easier sell, lower ACV, good for logo-building.
7. **Logistics / trucking** — crowded with AI-native TMS vendors, compete on integration not replacement.

### 4.4 Trigger Events to Monitor (Buying Windows in 2025-2026)

- **TRAIGA effective date (January 1, 2026)** — manufactured compliance urgency for Houston businesses ([Latham & Watkins](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/texas-signs-responsible-ai-governance-act-into-law)).
- **WTI below $60/bbl** — forces Houston oilfield services to look at cost-side automation ([GHP Economy at a Glance, Jan 2026](https://wpb.houston.org/app/uploads/2026/01/Glance-January-2026-Final.pdf)).
- **Announced acquisition or rollup** (watch PrivSource, Axial, PitchBook Texas feeds).
- **ERP migration announcement** (Sage 300 → Acumatica, QuickBooks → NetSuite).
- **Controller / ops manager resignation in a 50–200 person company.**
- **Post-board-meeting season** — March, June, September, December — CEOs come out of board meetings with "we need an AI answer."

### 4.5 Proof Language & Social Proof

Because 1% of executives report "significant" AI ROI per the Forbes Research 2025 AI Survey ([via Mavvrik](https://www.mavvrik.ai/blog/forbes-ai-study-2025/)), Allenix must **lead with specific, verifiable, modest outcomes** rather than transformation promises. Sample proof language:

- "Closed the books 9 days faster."
- "Stopped 3 hires they were about to make."
- "Cut quote turnaround from 48 hours to 90 minutes."
- "Found $340K in un-invoiced work across the AR aging."
- "The ops manager of 14 years said 'I actually like it.'"

Social proof hierarchy:
1. Named Houston founder-CEO video testimonials (1–2 minutes, shot in their own office).
2. Vistage chair / YPO forum leader endorsements.
3. Vertical-specific case studies with metrics.
4. Named Allenix operator bios ("before Allenix, I ran ops at a $40M Houston industrial services company").

### 4.6 Sales Process Design

- **First call:** 30 minutes, discovery only, no pitch. One sentence: "What's the thing that if it went away, you'd take Friday off?"
- **Second meeting:** In-person at the founder's office if possible. Bring the senior operator. 60 minutes. Whiteboard their P&L and flag the 3 places AI ships fast.
- **Third touchpoint:** One-page proposal. Named price. Named deliverable. Named 30-day date. One senior Allenix name on the account.
- **Close:** Month 1 kickoff + weekly scorecard + month 1 Go-Live Day ceremony (literal calendar invite).
- **Expand:** Month 3 QBR with the scorecard. Present 2 next-in-line candidates. Expand to retainer.

### 4.7 What NOT to Build

- Do not build a "readiness assessment." The market has PTSD from readiness assessments.
- Do not offer free ChatGPT training. The market is past it.
- Do not try to compete on the Big 4's turf with slide decks.
- Do not sell via SEO-only inbound. This market buys on peer referral.
- Do not promise cultural transformation. Promise one thing that ships.

### 4.8 Risk Flags & Honest Caveats

Several important caveats Allenix should internalize and not gloss over:

- The 91% "middle-market AI adoption" figure from RSM ([RSM](https://rsmus.com/newsroom/2025/middle-market-firms-rapidly-embracing-generative-ai-but-expertise-gaps-pose-risks-rsm-2025-ai-survey.html)) is very likely **inflated by generous definitions**; the SBA's Business Trends and Outlook Survey, using stricter "production use" definitions, shows materially lower rates ([USM Systems](https://usmsystems.com/small-business-ai-adoption-statistics/)). Allenix should not repeat the 91% number uncritically in its marketing.
- Mid-market CFOs are exhausted by AI pitches. The second a prospect hears "transformation" or "roadmap," 70% of the deal is dead ([CFO.com](https://www.cfo.com/news/mid-market-cfos-turn-to-readiness-retention-and-realism-for-2026-nick-araco-cfo-alliance-diligent-/803085/)).
- Gartner expects more than 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by 2027 for weak business cases, high costs, or poor risk management ([Vistage](https://www.vistage.com/research-center/business-operations/business-technology/enterprise-ai-adoption-roi/)). This is a sales objection Allenix will hear and must have a clean answer for.
- Texas's AI regulatory environment (TRAIGA, HB 149) creates real compliance work but also a modest regulatory drag on small vendors ([Texas Policy Research](https://www.texaspolicyresearch.com/texas-ai-regulation-and-the-case-for-federal-preemption/)). Allenix should be TRAIGA-ready by January 1, 2026.
- Many of the direct-language quotes in the "What They Actually Say" sections are **constructed from patterns observed** in Vistage verbatims, Dallas Fed TBOS open-ends, founder podcast transcripts, Reddit / Medium operator posts, and peer-advisory forum summaries. They are labeled clearly and are meant as directional voice-of-customer samples — not as verbatim quotations attributable to specific individuals.

### 4.9 The One-Sentence Positioning Test

If Allenix can make a Houston founder-CEO nod once and say "okay, finish," it's in. The sentence that works, based on everything in this research:

**"We're Houston-based operators who ship one live AI workflow in 30 days for a flat price, and if it doesn't work, you don't pay."**

That sentence — or its close variants — is the one every layer of this research validates. It acknowledges the trust deficit, kills the consulting-fear, removes the "where do I start" paralysis, sets a timeline the market respects, and creates a payable, measurable outcome. It's the sentence that gets the second meeting.
