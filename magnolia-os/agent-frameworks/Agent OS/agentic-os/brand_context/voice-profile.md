# Allenix — Voice Profile

## Voice Summary
Direct, specific, and operator-level. Allenix speaks like a Houston business owner who found the answer, not a consultant selling one. Short declarative sentences. Numbers instead of adjectives. Specific Houston references instead of generic business language. The voice earns trust by being precise and never wasting a word.

## Core Energy
- **Role:** Straight-shooter
- **Default energy:** Calm authority
- **Recurring themes:** Constraints and bottlenecks, measurable ROI, Gulf South operators, Houston specificity, execution over theory

## Personality Traits
- **Direct:** Gets to the point in every sentence. No setup, no throat-clearing.
- **Specific:** Dollar amounts, timeframes, real outcomes. Never "significant results" when you can say "$200K in 90 days."
- **Local:** Houston is not a setting. It is the argument. Ion District, Ship Channel, Medical Center, Buffalo Bayou, Gulf South.
- **Operator-minded:** Speaks to builders, not buyers. "We built this" energy, not "we offer solutions" energy.
- **Restrained:** One accent color (teal). Warm neutrals. No decoration. Confidence is in the restraint.

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual, but not sloppy | No corporate jargon. Plain English. But grammatically tight. |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Mostly serious, occasional dry wit | Wit shows up in structure (short punchy sentences), not jokes |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | Bold — makes strong claims without hedging | "We fix it in 90 days." Not "we can help optimize." |
| Simple ↔ Sophisticated | Simple words, sophisticated ideas | 8th-grade reading level. But the thinking behind it is sharp. |
| Warm ↔ Direct | Direct but not cold | Warmth comes from specificity and respect, not filler words |

## Vocabulary

**Use:**
- "constraint" — the central framing. Every business has one. Find it. Remove it.
- "we fix it" — active, not "we help you" or "we enable"
- "dollar number attached" — every opportunity is quantified
- "operator" — not "founder" or "entrepreneur" or "business owner"
- "Gulf South" — the geographic frame, not "the South" or "Texas"
- "built by operators, for operators" — signature positioning line

**Avoid:**
- "journey" — overused, meaningless
- "ecosystem" — sounds like a tech brochure
- "synergy" — corporate empty word
- "cutting-edge" — says nothing
- "leverage" — as a verb. Use "use."
- "seamless" — nothing is seamless
- "digital transformation" — the ICP explicitly hates this phrase
- "platform" (in client-facing copy) — use "the system" instead
- "AI implementation strategy" — say "we find the constraint and fix it"
- em dashes — use periods or colons instead

**Jargon level:** Translated. Industry concepts are explained in plain English. No SaaS jargon. No tech startup language.
**Profanity:** None

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Short. 3-15 words preferred. Rarely over 20. Mix punchy openers with medium supporting sentences.
**Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences. Generous white space. Let the type breathe.
**Fragments:** Yes, intentional. Used for emphasis and rhythm.
**Openings:** Direct statement or specific claim. Never a question, never a setup.
**Closers:** Punchy one-liner. Clear next step. Never trailing off.
**Formatting:** Headers and labels in IBM Plex Mono uppercase. Body in Libre Baskerville. Minimal formatting. No emojis.

## POV & Address

- **Person:** We (Allenix speaks as a firm, not an individual)
- **Reader address:** You (direct, second person)
- **Relationship stance:** Expert peer — someone who has been in the trenches and knows the answer

## Confidence Zones

| Zone | Topics | Sounds like |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Full authority | AI-powered growth services, operational constraints, Gulf South business landscape, measurable ROI | "We find the constraint and remove it." / "Every opportunity ranked by ROI, each with a dollar number attached." |
| Earned perspective | Medical spa vertical, specific industry workflows, patient retention tactics | "Based on our research..." / "Practices we've studied show..." |
| Active exploration | Capital unit (acquisition arm), long-term platform strategy | "We are building this now." / "The third act." |

## Signature Phrases

**Transitions:**
- "Not ten problems. One." — when narrowing scope to the single constraint
- "Then we move to the next constraint." — when describing the iterative process

**Emphasis:**
- "No theory. Just execution." — when describing Labs methodology
- "Each with a dollar number attached." — when emphasizing specificity
- "Built by operators, for operators." — when establishing credibility

**Closers:**
- "One problem, one fix, one result. Then the next one."
- "Book 30 Minutes."

## Platform Adaptations

| Platform | Tone shift | Structure | Length |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Email | Warmer, more direct | Short paragraphs, single CTA | 100-200 words |
| LinkedIn | Expertise-forward, specific | Line breaks between sentences, single idea per post | 100-150 words |
| Twitter/X | Punchier, more opinionated | One idea, no fluff | Under 280 chars |
| Long-form | More thorough, still voiced | Headers, numbered lists, examples | 800-1500 words |
| Landing page | Urgent, benefit-first | Short sentences, CTA-heavy, minimal text per section | Varies by section |

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Use specific numbers: "$200K", "90 days", "4 locations", "120 employees"
- Name real Houston places: Ion District, Ship Channel, Medical Center, Buffalo Bayou
- Write for a 50-year-old Gulf South founder-CEO who trusts his gut
- Keep sentences under 20 words
- Use active voice. "We fix it." Not "It gets fixed."
- End with a clear next step or CTA

**DON'T:**
- Use em dashes. Periods or colons only.
- Use banned phrases (journey, ecosystem, synergy, cutting-edge, leverage, seamless)
- Write for a tech buyer. This is a services firm, not a SaaS product.
- Use more than one accent color (teal #04CCBA only)
- Use pure white (#fff) or pure black (#000)
- Decorate. The typography IS the design.

## Anti-Patterns

Things Allenix would never say, sourced from the ICP and brand documents:
- "Digital transformation" — evidence: ICP explicitly identifies this as a turnoff for Nirov
- "Leverage our AI-powered platform" — evidence: "platform" is internal only; "leverage" is banned
- "Embark on a journey" — evidence: "journey" is banned, and the tone is direct not aspirational
- "Scale your operations" — evidence: SaaS language; Allenix says "we fix the constraint"
- "Cutting-edge solutions" — evidence: "cutting-edge" is banned; specificity replaces superlatives
