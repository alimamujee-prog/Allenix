# Allenix — Positioning

## Primary Angle

**Name:** The Local Constraint Remover
**Statement:** We find the single constraint costing your business the most time or money, and we fix it in 90 days. Built in Houston for Gulf South operators.
**Psychology:** The ICP ("Nirov") trusts people his peers trust, buys from conviction not features, and has been burned by coastal consultants who fly in, present, and leave. This angle wins by being local, specific, and outcome-committed. It answers his five-second homepage question ("Is this for me?") with yes: Gulf South, revenue range, measurable results.
**Headline direction:** "We find your constraint. We fix it in 90 days."
**Best for:** Gulf South mid-market services businesses ($5M-$50M revenue) that are operationally sophisticated but technologically behind. Founder-CEOs who trust their gut and need proof, not pitches.

## Market Assessment

**Sophistication:** Stage 4 — The market has heard every AI promise. Most buyers are skeptical of "AI-powered" anything. They need to see the mechanism and the result, not the category.
**Transformation:** From drowning in paperwork, manual processes, and gut-driven decisions to having the one highest-ROI fix deployed and running without you. Monday mornings back.
**Mechanism:** Three-phase system: Deep Discovery (map every workflow, rank by ROI), Identify the Constraint (one problem, quantified), Resolve It (build, train, stay until it runs). Every opportunity gets a dollar number attached.
**Primary alternative:** Do nothing. Keep running on gut and Excel. This is the most common path and the hardest to displace because it costs nothing until it costs everything.

## Competitive Landscape

**Competitors analyzed:**
- Big Four consultancies — "$500K engagement, 200-slide deck, no implementation"
- Boutique automation agencies — Often coastal, SaaS-native, speak tech not operator
- DIY tool purchases — Zapier, HubSpot, "AI-powered" things found on LinkedIn
- Nephew who "knows AI" — Free, unreliable, no methodology
- Do nothing — Status quo, gut and Excel

**Saturated claims (avoid):**
- "AI-powered transformation"
- "Scale your operations"
- "End-to-end digital solutions"
- "We partner with businesses to..."
- "Unlock growth through technology"

**White space identified:**
- **Local specificity.** No competitor is building for Gulf South operators specifically. The geographic frame is unclaimed territory.
- **Constraint-first framing.** Everyone sells transformation. Nobody sells "we find the one thing and fix it." The narrowing is the differentiation.
- **Operator-to-operator credibility.** Consultants sell expertise. Allenix sells having been in the trenches. The founder is a former operator, not a career consultant.
- **Dollar-number specificity.** "Every opportunity ranked by ROI, each with a dollar number attached." This is unusual in the space and builds immediate trust.

The Local Constraint Remover was selected because it directly addresses the ICP's five-second homepage question: "Is this for me?" No other angle combines local trust, process specificity, and time-bound commitment in a single statement.
